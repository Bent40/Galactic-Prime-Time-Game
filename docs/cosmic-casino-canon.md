## 1. Cosmology (owner-corrected, 2026-07-16)

- Roughly **once a quarter-millennium**, the bindings of the realms weaken and the gods
  get the opportunity to **claim divine favor from that realm's subjects**.
- This cycle it is **the humans, in the human realm, on Earth**.
- Realm-tied deities prepare **hidden champions** ahead of time; most of humanity is
  unaware anything is coming.
- When the world is thrown into the casino's games, **tables form** (see §2).

## 2. The tables

| Table | Who plays | What it is | Stakes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Normal** (low stakes) | Ordinary humanity | Humans vs monsters — world collapse, apocalypse, monsters everywhere | Bets on *whether* they survive, and *how well* |
| **VIP** | Champions + regular human "actors" | **Special games designed around what the gods found interesting during the quarter-millennium — mostly human pop culture.** 1 to a billion contestants, set by game type | Higher-stakes wagering; dealer tipping active |
| **VVIP** — nicknamed **Forsaken / Doomed games** | A lone champion | No help from the gods at all; everything solved alone | Much higher divinity involved; higher realms of power reachable; the sponsoring god's payout is much higher — but the champion is **doomed to complete the game fully alone** |

- **Forsaken games are god-initiated (owner, 2026-07-16):** story-wise the champion is
  **chosen by the gods to overcome a trial bigger than others** — it is *the gods' way of
  going all-in*, never the champion's refusal of patronage. (Marcus: Plutus went all-in;
  consent not included.)

- **Tipping the dealer:** at every non-Forsaken table, gods can tip the dealer to help
  their luck — **boons, buffs, items**, or hindering others with **trials, monsters,
  curses**. (This is the wager/patron-influence mechanism.)
- Bible detail that may still apply inside the Forsaken frame (unconfirmed): betting
  opens at 10× stakes; personal challenges begin day 3, apostle challenges day 8.

## 3. The divinity economy (the point of it all)

- The more **currency** a god wins, the higher their **divinity** — and the more
  **central their religion will be for the next 250 years**.
- **Winners of games can take some of the currency being bet around → gain divinity →
  join the table as gamblers.** (Mortal→god pipeline is canon.)
- **The final winner of the tables decides how history is shaped for the next 250 years
  and how the apocalypse is remembered.** That is the source of **every legend, idea,
  and imagination humanity ever created** — it all stems from these games.

## 4. Who runs the games

- The games are controlled **not by AI but by fallen gods** — gods who went **bankrupt**:
  **forgotten religions**. Explicit owner note: *"we can make up whatever we want here —
  relics, shapes, ideas, names, anything goes."* (Free design space; no
  mythology-accuracy debt.)
- **Momus** hosts/announces (bible): pink tuxedo "as if a flamingo decided it wanted to
  be human," never breathes between sentences, unseen laughing audience, sign-off
  *"This is Momus. Stay tuned!"*
- **Arena design rule** (bible): each arena is shaped by the **judge/director god's
  psyche** — symbolic, psychological, surreal; never just physical.
- Hard rule (bible): gods are **morally alien throughout** — addicted, bored, starving
  for spectacle. No redemption, no hidden benevolence, nothing to twirl a mustache.

## 5. The novel's three protagonists (plot-protected — context only)

1. **Marcus ("Mark")** — down-on-his-luck man, **tricked by a blind and naive god into
   playing as his champion**, landing in a **Forsaken game** so the god earns a higher
   payout if he wins. **Patron: Plutus.** The game itself is **controlled by Enki**
   (Mesopotamian), who is trying to teach Marcus how to survive and win **while
   remaining impartial**. (The Ch3 pool figure — bearded man rising from the water,
   fish, sea-salt — is Enki; the Ch2 cliffhanger voice resolves there. Inference to
   confirm: Paul, the blind man who gave Marcus the ticket, reads as Plutus.)
2. **Viola** — 40-year-old Navy commander placed in a **large Star-Wars-like fleet
   game** as a soldier; climbs the imperial military from scratch; falls in love with
   one of her soldiers; an **"NPC" commander is directed to be rid of her** and ambushes
   her during a rebel engagement; she escapes with her girlfriend and crashes on a
   **mechanical planet** whose machine race believes in **Deus Ex Machina, the director
   god of her game**, who left a prophecy of a flesh-and-blood messiah. They **reforge
   her into a machine** — keeping her heart, her brain, and a **missing eye she refused
   to repair** so she remembers what the empire did to her — and implant the planet's
   core, **a miniature sun, in her chest** (radiates yellow). She leaves the dying
   planet with her beloved's body. Her game lets her **cash out kills for divine
   grace**; a revival potion costs far more lives than the rebel army holds — so she
   **decimates everyone, rebel and imperial**, sits on an empty throne, buys the
   potion… and her beloved returns as a **soulless ghoul**. This is Viola's rise as an
   **agent against the gods** — someone who went through **apotheosis**.
3. **Third protagonist (in progress)** — a game of **a million people** where one is
   chosen to become a **sentient zombie**: a human who can infect others on bite into
   bloodthirsty cannibalistic monsters. His goal: wipe out humanity. Their goal: find
   and kill him. Brains-type character; a **definite villain in his own story** — who
   eventually **joins the protagonists' party as a necessary force**.

Design takeaways for the game (not plot): VIP games span genres (a fleet war game, a
million-player hidden-traitor zombie game) — the table structure is built to host
*different games of different shapes inside one apocalypse*. Apotheosis has narrative
precedent as tragedy. NPC actors can be directed against specific players by the house.

## 6. Bible material that still stands (V0.2 bible, condensed)

- **Tone/voice:** LitRPG / dark fantasy / cosmic horror / survival; cynical, satirical,
  bleak — "Squid Game meets Dark Souls." No hero power fantasy, no divine redemption,
  no chosen one. Narration = sardonic deadpan inner monologue; deliberate slow-mundane
  build to an inconvenienced punchline. POV (first vs third-limited) still open.
- **Marcus texture:** 28, laid off after 10 years via form letter; "mediocrity"; brand =
  golden rectangle on the chest, champion **#2,568,976**; opening survival odds
  **1:259** shown beside a poker-chip stack; zone literally labeled *"Forsaken Shopping
  District."*
- **Stage 1 setting:** hometown transformed into a magical arena; real locations become
  fantasy equivalents (supermarket→general store, library→magical archive,
  school→training hall, park→monster territory); clear districts by defeating major
  monsters; red sky, collapsed buildings, blackouts.
- **Monsters:** collateral **followers of gods** dragged into the framework. Three
  states: **Insane Followers** (hostile, XP), **Sane Followers** (aware —
  traders/allies/quest-givers), **Worshipped Creatures** (revered beings, moral
  dilemmas). **Shadow people:** pantomime grief, swarm as invisible dragging weight in
  darkness, repelled by light, hide from the minimap. **Offspring of Asag:** rock-pile
  children of the mountains × Asag/Asakku (Mesopotamian disease-demon); dumb, immune to
  disease/pain, devastatingly strong; pleasant smell; the template for
  mythology-sourced monster design.
- **Loot:** **God Relics** (true divine artifacts, jackpot-rare) and **Follower Relics**
  (objects empowered by faith — Infinite Rice Sack, Ghost Warding Torch, Rusted Legion
  Spear). Hard rule: **loot must feel like remnants of belief**, never generic stat
  sticks.
- **HUD/system:** chipper intrusive personality; congratulates tragedies; pun-laden
  voiced bestiary; mocking achievements ("Challenged," "Intoxicated," "Unlucky") — but
  can read the room and dim politely. Health bar + **Focus bar** (function TBD);
  minimap (red dots; hiders vanish); odds display; locked **patron** (praying hands)
  and **comms** (speech bubble) icons; system text renders Greek then shuffles to
  English. Planned tabs: Patron, Party, **Current Bet** (live wagers affecting you).
- **Supporting cast:** Larry (small perpetually-frowning bartender); **Paul** (blind,
  skeletal, crumpled expensive suit, formally strange speech, uncannily perceptive;
  gave Marcus the flash-paper ticket from an unnamed "friend").
- **Chapter beats:** Ch1 "Mediocrity" (pub → Paul → ticket → E356 → branding → Forsaken
  announcement → odds 1:259); Ch2 "Duck and Hide" (shadow-people gauntlet → Asag
  offspring → ribs shattered → a voice finishes his thought); Ch3 "Broken" fragment
  (wakes in the public pool; Enki has realigned his ribs — "rest another day").
- **Collaboration rules (owner's own):** present options, the author decides; keep the
  cynical grounded voice; check hard rules before proposing; deliberate stylistic
  choices are features — verify intent before flagging.

## 7. Corrections ledger — where 2026-07-16 supersedes the bible

1. **Cosmology mechanism:** not "a realm is selected as host for an apocalypse show" —
   the realm bindings *weaken* on the ~250-year cycle and the gods claim divine favor;
   the whole world drops into the games at multiple tables simultaneously.
2. **Table taxonomy:** Normal / VIP / VVIP-Forsaken replaces the bible's Mass/VIP/War
   framing. (War games like Viola's now read as a *genre of VIP game*, not a tier.)
3. **Marcus's patron:** the bible's "degenerate gambling addict who constantly
   sabotages" is superseded by **Plutus — blind and naive — who *tricked* Marcus** into
   the Forsaken game for a higher payout. Whether the "patron never redeems" hard rule
   survives this softer characterization: **owner to confirm.**
4. **Who runs a game:** now explicit — **fallen (bankrupt) gods of forgotten
   religions**, with full creative license. Marcus's game runner: **Enki**.
5. **The endgame economy:** winners→gamblers pipeline and the final winner **shaping
   250 years of history and the apocalypse's memory** are new canon; the bible's
   "Marcus becomes unwagerable" north star was not restated — treat as **owner to
   reconcile** (unwagerable exit vs divinity buy-in are different doors out).
