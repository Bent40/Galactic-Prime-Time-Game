## 1 · The core framework — how to *imbue* an emotion (the repeatable part)

Every parameter of music is a lever on two dials: **valence** (pleasant↔unpleasant) and
**energy** (calm↔intense). The method:

> **1.** Name the target emotion in **two words** ("grief + hope", "triumphant dread").
> **2.** Point **all levers the same way** (table below).
> **3.** Write a 4-chord loop, then **bend one chord** (borrowed minor iv is the workhorse).
> **4.** Add **one tension device**: ostinato or pedal point.
> **5.** Arrange the **3-act arc**: Setup (solo, intimate) → Build (engine + layers) →
> Climax (everything, once).
> **6.** **Break exactly one lever at the peak** — key lift, Picardy third, or the full
> ensemble crash. The broken expectation *is* the emotional moment.

**Lever table:**

| Lever | → bright / hopeful / intense | → dark / sad / intimate |
|---|---|---|
| Key / mode | major; **Lydian** = wonder; Mixolydian = heroic | minor; **Dorian** = hopeful-sad; **Phrygian** = menace |
| Tempo | fast = drive, joy | slow = dignity, grief, calm |
| Contour | rising = anticipation, triumph | falling = sorrow, release |
| Register | high = light | low = weight *(your voice lives here — asset, not bug)* |
| Rhythm | driving ostinato = momentum | rubato / pulseless = suspension, grief |
| Dynamics | sudden loud = punctuation | hushed = intimacy |
| Texture | thick = grandeur | sparse = isolation, vulnerability |
| Timbre | brass = grandeur; full strings = sweep | solo instrument = intimacy; woodwinds = warmth |

**Tension devices** (pick ONE per piece while learning): pedal point (hold bass, move chords,
release = payoff) · ostinato (loop a figure, re-colour harmony around it) · suspension (hold
a note into the next chord, resolve down = yearning) · modulation (step-up lift = final-chorus
surge) · drop-to-solo (intimacy before the re-entry).

**Emotional chord moves that punch above their weight:** minor iv in a major key
(bittersweet); Picardy third (minor piece ends major = sudden hope); constraining the palette
(world.execute(me) keeps i, iv, v *all minor* = uniform cold).

## 2 · The three composers — what to actually steal

### Mili (Cassie Wei + Yamato Kasai) — *contrast, character, hybrid*
- **The arc IS the song:** soft piano open → layered escalation → dense rock/orchestral peak.
- **Two worlds per cue:** one acoustic colour (piano/strings/chimes) + one electronic
  (pad/programmed beat), sharing the arrangement.
- **Escalate by re-scoring, not replacing:** Library of Ruina boss tiers reuse the calm piano
  motif and add a soaring violin as victory nears. One motif, N intensities — **this is the
  model for our game's music.**
- **Every theme has a subject** — a specific character's feeling ("String Theocracy" =
  Angela's yearning). A cue *about something* reads as emotional; a generic bed doesn't.
- **Sweet-vs-dark dissonance:** pretty melody/timbre over heavy content (or inverse).
- **Lyrics from one extended metaphor:** a whole text derived from one vocabulary domain
  (code, gravity, geometry). Forces coherence.

### Kevin Penkin — *timbre, restraint, meaning-in-orchestration*
- **Score the subtext:** one sentence naming the feeling ("tiny people, enormous unknown"),
  *then* choose sounds to serve it. Translation, not decoration.
- **Soloist ecosystem:** instead of one lush pad, several *independent* solo lines that only
  occasionally converge — lonelier, more alive. (His Made in Abyss chamber-of-19 trick.)
- **Fuse acoustic + synth into ONE sound** (EQ/reverb them together until the seam vanishes).
- **Field recordings as instruments** — record the world with a phone, process into texture.
- **Small forces, big room:** emptiness = vastness. Resist filling every frequency.
- **Voice without words:** invented syllables ("Hanezeve Caradhina") = pure emotion, no
  semantics. Cheapest profound trick available to us.
- **Character-instrument duets:** Florence = piano, Krish = cello; the relationship arc is
  literally a duet. Directly reusable for party members.
- **Workflow:** sketch ~80% in the DAW → the last 20% is massaged with real performance
  (even solo: re-record one live element over the mock-up and hear the lift).

### Hiroyuki Sawano — *energy management, the earned drop*
- **Quiet-then-huge, on purpose:** solo piano/voice open → long build → a **break**
  (near-silence, one bar) → full ensemble + vocal slam. His own words: choruses and breaks
  "expand the sound" when a section feels flat.
- **One hook, reprised BIGGER, not different.** Escalate a single melody; don't write five.
- **Ostinato = the engine.** 1–2 bar figure loops under everything; harmony swells on top.
- **Minor-key gravity, relative-major lift** [interp.]: build in dark minor, brighten the
  drop toward relative major = tension → euphoria.
- **Modulate up to re-energize the final repeat** (UNICORN crosses four keys).
- **Three families, revealed one at a time:** orchestra / band / electronics — hold them low
  and quiet in the build so the drop reads as an explosion, not a wall you leaned on.
- **The voice is the climax layer** — full lead vocal (or wordless choir) arrives at the peak,
  last and highest-impact.
- **Contrast > complexity.** Before adding notes, ask: can I make the *previous* section
  emptier instead?

### Where all three agree (= our house style)
1. The **soft→huge arc** with restraint before release.
2. **Hybrid acoustic + electronic** as one fused colour.
3. **The voice as an instrument** — wordless/invented syllables at the emotional peak.
4. **One motif re-scored across intensities** — which is also exactly what a game needs.
5. **Contrast is the emotion.** Density, dynamics, and register are managed, not maxed.

## 3 · Vocal technique — the references, adapted to a low male voice

### What the references teach (technique, not pitch)
- **Ado:** the two pillars — (1) *restrained thin softness* in verses (power held in reserve),
  (2) growl/distortion dropped **instantly** back to clean. Register shifts as *emotional*
  shifts; consonants as percussion. Mix-voice power without strain.
- **Cassie Wei:** breathy "sweet" timbre as a *setup* for dark content; **self-duets in two
  registers** (Ga1ahad: low-serious vs high-bright = two characters, one singer — perfect for
  us); precise, editing-forward tuning as texture (Vocaloid heritage).
- **Hana-tan:** the cover-singer discipline — **re-colour the voice per song** instead of
  singing everything one way; live = studio-quality control.
- **MAPHRA:** harsh scream ↔ clean toggle; emotion-first delivery ("you're connecting with
  the emotion, not hearing me sing").

### The low-range adaptation strategy (the actual plan)
1. **Transpose, don't octave-flip.** Start female-key songs **a 4th–5th down**, then tune
   by **tessitura** (where *most* of the song sits), not by the top note. Method: sing it an
   octave down, raise semitone-by-semitone until it sits best.
2. **Octave-displace inside the song** — keep verses in rich chest, drop only peak phrases.
3. **Build mix voice** (the real unlock, months-scale): light sirens + lip-trill glides
   through the break, 3–4×/week. Keep fold closure, let resonance rise, **add zero air**.
4. **Your low range is a feature:** chest resonance, relaxed speech-like placement, dark
   warmth (the Johnny Cash mechanism). When a bright reference comes down, trade her
   brightness for *your* intimacy — don't imitate.
5. **Growl/scream is pitch-independent** — false-cord/fry mechanics learned separately, at
   easy pitches, never by straining true folds.
6. **Health floor:** 10–15 min warm-up (hum → lip trills → straw phonation → sirens), hydrate,
   never force volume, cool down on low hums.

**Low-male models to study (bridge artists):** **Hozier** (low voice mixing UP, connected not
thin — your #1), **Johnny Cash** (owning depth via resonance), **Sinatra/Nat King Cole**
(phrasing + breath), Matt Berninger (full expression in a narrow low range), Eddie Vedder
(chest power), Elvis (baritone↔tenor movement).

### Range analysis — MEASURED 2026-07-21 (owner's clips, YIN pitch-track)

| Metric | Result |
|---|---|
| Chest floor (sustained) | **D2** |
| Chest ceiling (connected, no crack — voluntary stop) | **C4** |
| Falsetto span | **A3 → D#5** (light) |
| Tessitura (song, p25–p75) | **C3–D#3**, centre **C#3** |
| Song range actually used | F2 → G3 |
| Passaggio / mix zone (chest-top ∩ falsetto-floor) | **A3–C4** |

**Voice type: bass-baritone, leaning bass** — D2 sustained floor + comfort centred C#3.
The D#5 falsetto extension is a genuine asset (Penkin-style wordless top layer is available
*today*; no mix voice needed for it).

**Personal transposition defaults:**
- **Female refs (Ado / Cassie Wei / Hana-tan):** sing the melody an octave down, then nudge
  the key ±2 st so phrase peaks land ≤ G3 (or deliberately in A3–C4 as the "reach" colour).
- **High male refs (Yosh etc.):** transpose down ~5–7 st; climaxes land F3–G3.
- **Writing original melodies:** verses orbit **B2–E3**; rises to F3–G3; three climax moves —
  full chest G3–C4 (the reach), flip to falsetto A3–D5 (colour), or octave-drop for weight.
- **Default writing keys: D minor or E minor** (majors F / G) — melody naturally spans
  A2–G3. Use these for the Week-2/4 exercises.
- **Mix-voice training target = A3–C4** — sirens/lip-trills bridge exactly the zone where
  chest and falsetto already overlap.

*(Method: single-take phone clips, YIN tracker, median-smoothed; percentile stats are robust
to octave blips. Falsetto top is a light D#5, not a belt.)*

**Voice profile v1 — baseline for monthly re-measurement (2026-07-21):**

| Metric | Value | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Vibrato | none (straight tone; drift 3.5 Hz, aperiodic) | normal untrained; stylistically fine |
| Intonation scatter (MAD) | **39 cents** | loosest metric — main practice target |
| Onset scoop (median) | **+428 c, 71% of onsets** | approaches notes from far below — drill target |
| Phrase length | median 4.3 s, max 7.9 s | solid breath support already |
| Jitter / shimmer | 0.41% / 4.1% (indicative) | stable phonation |
| Chest timbre (slide) | H1-H2 ≈ 0.7 dB, HNR 9.6 dB | firm, pressed low register |
| Falsetto timbre | H1-H2 ≈ 24 dB, HNR 15.8 dB | pure flute falsetto |
| Song timbre | H1-H2 ≈ 8.8 dB | moderately breathy comfort tone (on-brand) |
| Singer's formant | −17 dB ratio; no 2.8–3.2 kHz cluster | not yet developed (expected) |
| **Registers at A3–C4** | chest 6.8 vs falsetto 9.1 dB H1-H2; HNR ≈ equal | **already converging — small mix gap** |

Priority order from the data: **1)** onset precision (land the note centre from the attack),
**2)** drone/keyboard pitch-matching to shrink scatter 39→~20 c, **3)** light sirens through
A3–C4 (the registers nearly meet — cheapest win). Caveats: phone mic + AGC, a-cappella,
small n; indicative baselines, not clinical.

## 5 · The 6-week plan → one finished piece by Aug 31

~1 hr/day. **Every week ends in an exported artifact** — finishing trains the ear faster
than tutorials. Daily: 10 min ear training + (from wk 2) the vocal warm-up.

| Wk | Focus | Deliverable (exported) |
|---|---|---|
| **1** (Jul 21–27) | FL + LABS/BBCSO/Vital installed; the four windows; first loop | 8-bar loop, LABS Soft Piano, one 4-chord progression |
| **2** (Jul 28–Aug 3) | Diatonic chords; **minor iv**; Picardy third; start daily vocal warm-ups | TWO contrasting loops — one "hopeful", one "grieving" (prove you can steer) |
| **3** (Aug 4–10) | Melody & contour; solo-instrument intimacy; find your keys (range clips → analysis) | 16-bar melody + accompaniment; your transposition table |
| **4** (Aug 11–17) | ONE tension device (ostinato/pedal); the 3-act build | 60–90 s instrumental with a real build and drop |
| **5** (Aug 18–24) | **Wordless/phonetic vocal** over the build (Penkin trick — no lyrics needed); comp takes; mix basics (volume/pan/EQ/light compression) | vocal-over-instrumental rough mix |
| **6** (Aug 25–31) | Reference-mix vs one Mili/Penkin/Sawano track; fix the two worst things; add the ONE broken lever at the peak | 🏁 **the piece** — 1–2 min, WAV+MP3 |

**Definition of "feels okay" (honesty gate, so we can call it honestly):** a listener can name
the intended emotion unprompted · the climax lands *because* the build withheld · the vocal
sits in a key chosen for the owner's range · exported, plays start-to-finish, no placeholder
silence. Not required: mixing polish, lyrics, more than one piece.

The game keeps its **chiptune baseline** (the "watching the broadcast" frame) and deploys
cinematic music as the **rare contrast weapon** (`making-art-and-music.md` §0/§2.6 — the
orchestral sting under an execution). Everything above is what makes that sting land. The
