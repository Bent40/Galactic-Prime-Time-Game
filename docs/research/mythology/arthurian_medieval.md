## Shape of the tradition (calibration note)
This is a LITERARY legend cycle with almost no cultic history. Per the frozen **Väinämöinen calibration anchor** (national-literary fame without worship = influence 1, recognition 3), nearly every figure here scores **influence 1** while the famous ones run **recognition 4–5**. That is the expected fame-without-worship shape: household names on **Normal tables** as collector's champions, not VVIP high-rollers (the Zeus lesson — recognition 5, influence 2 still lands VIP; here influence 1 lands Normal). Table tier is dominated by worship-wealth, not fame.

**The ONE exception — Morgan le Fay sets the influence ceiling at 3.** The Glastonbury Goddess Temple has been a **registered place of worship since 18 June 2003** (goddesstemple.co.uk; WRSP / wrldrels.org). Its principal deities are **the Lady of Avalon — explicitly identified as Morgen la Fey — and the Nine Morgens** (healing goddesses of Avalon). That is a small **organized revival** (§3.1 influence 3, Ásatrú-scale), so Morgan alone carries influence 3 and `depiction_risk: low` (revival worship exists; depict respectfully). This REFINES the census's coarse `living` flag: `living` in the rubric is reserved for major/significant living practice (Ganesha, Amaterasu); a single revival temple maps to the revival tier (`low`), not `living`. Under depiction-policy v2 it is DATA, not a gate, either way.

## HEROES & LEGENDARY FIGURES

### §Arthur — King Arthur (hero, patron_capable:false)
The once-and-future king. Drew the sword from the stone to prove his kingship (Malory I); received Excalibur from the Lady of the Lake; founded the Round Table fellowship and its Pentecost Oath; fell at the last battle of **Camlann**, dealing Mordred a death-wound and taking his own; borne to Avalon by Morgan and the queens. **LEGEND-FIRST vs D-4:** the debated 5th–6th c. British war-leader (Nennius, *Annales Cambriae*) is a genuinely borderline historicity case — treated per instructions as OUT (the historical man is not the entity); the **LEGENDARY** once-and-future king IS the entity. Influence 1 (no worship), recognition 5 (household name). Casino: retired champion brought back as a table boss / contestant-legend; the sleeping-king motif = a lose-but-revive-stronger mechanic. *Sources: Malory I, XXI; Geoffrey, Historia; Mabinogion (Culhwch).*

### §Merlin (spirit, patron_capable:true — sorcerous/prophet patron)
Prophet-enchanter of demonic parentage: the medieval *Prose Merlin* / Robert de Boron tradition makes him an intended Antichrist — begotten on a virgin by a devil — but baptized at birth, keeping the diabolic gift of seeing past and future while escaping the Devil's purpose (verified: medievalists.net; Binghamton history journal). He engineers Arthur's begetting (disguising Uther as Gorlois at Tintagel), builds the king, makes the Round Table and the Siege Perilous, then is undone by his own pupil **Nimue/Viviane**, who coaxes his secrets from him and seals him alive under rock / in an invisible tower (verified: Lady of the Lake sources; TV Tropes/Myth index cross-checked to Vulgate). Patron-capable as a sorcerous patron whose boons are FOREKNOWLEDGE, not stats. **Forsaken-host candidate** (a mage who staked all and was unmade). Influence 1, recognition 5. *Sources: Robert de Boron/Prose Merlin; Geoffrey, Vita Merlini; Malory I, IV.*

### §Morgan le Fay (spirit, patron_capable:true — fae-sorceress patron)
Shape-shifting sorceress-queen of Avalon, Arthur's half-sister, healer AND destroyer. In Malory she steals **the scabbard of Excalibur** in revenge for her lover Accolon (slain by Arthur), and throws it into a deep lake — lost forever, which dooms Arthur to bleed at Camlann (verified: Wikipedia/Excalibur, one level down to Malory IV). Yet she is among the queens who ferry the dying Arthur to Avalon. The ONLY entity here with living worshippers (Glastonbury Goddess Temple, above). Patron-capable as a vengeful fae; casino VIP table-boss + Forsaken-host candidate. Influence 3 (revival), recognition 4, table VIP. Note the scholarly-DEBATED link to the Irish Morrígan — left `syncretic_group: null` (unsettled). *Sources: Malory IV, XXI; Geoffrey, Vita Merlini (the Nine Sisters of Avalon); Glastonbury Goddess Temple.*

### §Lady of the Lake — Nimue/Viviane (spirit, patron_capable:true — fae arms-dealer of destiny)
Water-fay whose role-set (verified, Wikipedia/EBSCO → Malory/Vulgate): **gives Arthur Excalibur**, **imprisons Merlin**, **fosters and raises Lancelot** in her submerged realm after his father's death, and helps bear the dying Arthur to Avalon. Malory uses two Ladies — the first (unnamed) gives the sword and is later beheaded at court; the second, **Nimue/Nenyve**, becomes the true Lady of the Lake and the court's protector. Folded into one playable identity; Nimue/Viviane/Ninianne are scribal variants, not a cross-tradition group. Patron-capable fae arms-dealer: boons arrive as a perfectly-timed weapon or a raised champion, always a loan against a destined return. Influence 1, recognition 4. *Sources: Malory I–IV; Lancelot-Grail (Vulgate).*

### §Lancelot (hero)
Lancelot du Lac, greatest knight of the world, raised by the Lady of the Lake; his adultery with Queen Guinevere is the flaw that cracks the fellowship and ignites the civil war. Father of Galahad. Non-patron contestant-legend; epithet templates 'the Peerless' and 'the Fallen Best' (your greatest strength becomes the thing that dooms an ally). Influence 1, recognition 4. *Sources: Vulgate Lancelot; Malory VI, XVIII–XX.*

### §Guinevere (hero)
Arthur's queen; the love triangle with Lancelot is the fault line that burns Camelot. The near-burning and Lancelot's rescue is the recurring set-piece. Prize/royalty NPC and love-triangle event seed (epithet 'the Kindling'). Influence 1, recognition 4. *Sources: Chrétien, Knight of the Cart; Malory XX.*

### §Gawain (hero)
Arthur's nephew, knight of courtesy; in Malory his strength **waxes toward noon and wanes after** (a solar trait — a clean in-run power-clock mechanic). Central to *Sir Gawain and the Green Knight*: he alone takes up the Green Knight's beheading game and keeps the appointment. Brother of Mordred; later feuds with Lancelot over his kin's deaths. Non-patron; the designated challenger paired with the Green Knight boss. Influence 1, recognition 4. *Sources: Sir Gawain and the Green Knight; Malory (Gawain's solar strength).*

### §Galahad (hero)
The sinless knight, son of Lancelot; the destined Grail-winner who alone may sit the Siege Perilous (his name appears on it) and achieve the Holy Grail, then is raptured out of the story at his triumph. The 'perfect run' seed: a flawless clear unlocks the Grail-tier reward and a peak-exit payout. Influence 1, recognition 3. *Sources: Vulgate Queste del Saint Graal; Malory XIII–XVII.*

## ANTAGONISTS & BEASTS

### §Mordred (villain)
Arthur's incest-born son and the arch-traitor; seizes the throne and queen in Arthur's absence, forcing the last battle of Camlann where father and son kill each other (the double-kill). Boss seed with a DISCOVERABLE win condition, never a damage race: he is invulnerable while the fellowship he has subverted stays divided — heal the schism first, then survive the mutual death-blow. Influence 1, recognition 3. *Sources: Geoffrey, Historia; Malory XXI.*

### §The Green Knight (spirit) — the showcase discoverable-win-condition BOSS
A holly-bearing green giant who rides into Camelot on New Year's and proposes a **beheading game**: a volunteer may strike him one free blow, on condition of receiving the return blow a year hence at the Green Chapel. Gawain beheads him; the Green Knight PICKS UP HIS OWN HEAD and rides off. At the return, revealed as **Bertilak de Hautdesert**, he feigns twice and on the third stroke leaves only a nick — punishment for Gawain concealing the lady's charmed girdle during the nested **exchange-of-winnings** wager (verified: Wikipedia/SGGK, ancient-origins, LitCharts → the poem). THE PERFECT discoverable-win-condition boss: the win is to keep the covenant HONESTLY at the return (stand still, no hidden charm), not to out-damage him — cheating (stacking secret protection) takes the wound; faith earns the survivable nick. Carries **luck_gambling** (his covenant + the exchange-of-winnings bet are the tradition's clearest gambling resonance) → casino nobility / Forsaken host. Influence 1, recognition 3. *Sources: Sir Gawain and the Green Knight; Irish antecedent — Bricriu's Feast / Cú Chulainn — noted, not merged.*

### §Questing Beast — Glatisant (beast)
A cross-animal chimera: **head like a serpent, body like a leopard, buttocks like a lion, feet like a hart, and in its belly a noise as of thirty couple of hounds questing** (verified verbatim: Wikipedia/Questing Beast → Malory). Its name (Glatisant, from Fr. *glapissant*, 'yelping') comes from that belly-noise, which originates in a symbolic dream (Gesta Regum Anglorum). Pursued futilely by King Pellinore and his line, finally by Sir Palamedes — but canonically never caught. Chase-boss seed: cannot be killed by damage; the win is to out-endure or trap it; the belly-noise is an audio tell. Influence 1, recognition 2 (rare board). *Sources: Malory; Post-Vulgate.*

## ARTIFACTS (item-seeds)

### §Excalibur (artifact, recognition 5)
The lake-given blade. Its real treasure is the **SCABBARD**: while worn, the bearer receives no bleeding wound — Merlin chides Arthur that the sheath is the greater treasure (verified: Wikipedia/Excalibur → Malory). Morgan steals the scabbard and throws it in a lake; it is never found, leaving Arthur mortally vulnerable at Camlann. Item-seed: attuned blade (worth-gated) + a scabbard that negates the bleeding condition, STEALABLE mid-run by a rival patron; returned to the water at the end (Bedivere's throw). PLACEHOLDER numbers. *Sources: Malory I, IV, XXI.*

### §The Holy Grail (artifact, recognition 5 — OWNER-REVIEW FLAG)
The cup of the Last Supper; the ultimate unattainable prize, achieved only by the sinless (Galahad, with Percival and Bors attending) and ruinous to the unworthy who seek it flawed. Jackpot / perfect-run seed with a peak-exit rapture payoff. **SENSITIVITY:** a PD literary object BUT entangled with **living Christian eucharistic symbolism** — `depiction_risk: low` is set deliberately to carry the census's OWNER-REVIEW recommendation before any irreverent gambling framing (not because the object is cultically worshipped). Influence 1. *Sources: Chrétien, Perceval; Vulgate Queste; Malory XIII–XVII.*

### §The Round Table (artifact, recognition 4)
A table with no head — equality by design. Malory: it belonged to Uther, passed to King Leodegrance, and came to Arthur as **Guinevere's dowry** with 100 knights; Merlin made it and it seats 150; Arthur's knights swear the **Pentecost Oath** (give mercy to him that asks it, flee treason and outrage, succour ladies and gentlewomen on pain of death), renewed every Pentecost (verified: Wikipedia/Knights of the Round Table → Malory III). Item-seed: fellowship party-buff (pooled bonus, flat threat), the Oath as an opt-in run-modifier, and a diegetic pun — it can BE the VIP gaming table. Contains the Siege Perilous. *Sources: Malory III; Wace, Roman de Brut (the round shape ends precedence quarrels).*

### §The Siege Perilous (artifact, recognition 2)
The deadly empty seat at the Round Table, made/reserved by Merlin for the one destined Grail-knight; **fatal to anyone unworthy who sits in it**, and Galahad's name appears on it when he arrives on Whitsunday to claim it (verified: Wikipedia/Siege Perilous → Malory XIII). Cursed-relic / Forsaken-hazard item-seed and an all-in staking object: sit worthy, take the pot; sit unworthy, the house takes you. The rim naming the destined = a worth-check tell. *Sources: Vulgate Queste; Malory XIII.*

## EXCLUSIONS & JUDGMENT CALLS (D-4 and sensitivity)

**Verified historical humans with legend cycles — EXCLUDED as entities (D-4), story-only material:** Charlemagne and Roland (Roland = the historical Hruodland, 778 Roncevaux; the *Song of Roland* and the wider Matter of France stay as story-only material — **Durendal** could resurface later as a stand-alone artifact only if the owner rules 'sword-of-a-historical-owner' admissible); El Cid (Rodrigo Díaz de Vivar, *Cantar de mio Cid*); Dietrich von Bern (legendary mask of Theodoric the Great); Tannhäuser (historical minnesinger; the Venusberg legend is story-only); Lady Godiva; Hereward the Wake. None of these became entity records.

**Borderline judged IN as LEGEND-FIRST:** King Arthur himself (historicity genuinely debated → treated as the LEGENDARY king, per instruction; the historical war-leader stays out); Robin Hood, Ogier the Dane, Prester John, Melusine (no verified historical individual behind the legend, or pure fairy legend) — of the shortlist here, all 15 selected are legend-first with no historical-person problem.

**Sensitivity exclusions (NOT D-4):** the Wandering Jew legend excluded on antisemitic-deployment-history grounds (unsuitable for casino content); William Tell left off as disputed-historicity Alpine national material. The Holy Grail retained (indispensable, recognition 5) but carries an explicit owner-review flag for its living-Christian symbolism.

**Nothing in this tradition is culturally closed or restricted** — all core texts are public, published, public-domain literature.
