# Greek & Roman mythology — Wave 2 extraction dossier

**Tradition:** `greek_roman` · **Batch date:** 2026-07-18 · **Researcher:** Wave-2 extraction agent (ruled pipeline)
**Census inputs honored:** influence_ceiling 2 · recognition_ceiling 5 · source_quality 5 · depiction_risk none tradition-wide (Hellenic revival scored below the influence-3 bar per the frozen Wave-0 calibration; Hecate, the one modern-devotion outlier, was not selected this wave).

## 1. Tradition overview — the casino angle

Greek/Roman is the game's flagship "rich-and-famous-but-broke" pantheon: the highest recognition ceiling in the census (5) welded to the lowest influence ceiling (2). At the Cosmic Casino they are old money whose bank collapsed twenty-five cycles ago — everyone knows their faces, the VIP audience cheers their names, and their actual bankroll seats them at VIP, never VVIP (the frozen Zeus calibration lesson: fame does not buy VVIP). This makes them the canon's ideal *first* patron roster: maximum audience draw, no depiction gates, fully public-domain corpus.

The corpus is also uniquely casino-shaped at the source level: the cosmos itself was **divided by casting lots** (Iliad 15.187–193 — Zeus, Poseidon, and Hades literally drew for sky, sea, and underworld); the Fates were **cheated once, with wine** (Aeschylus, Eumenides 723–728); Hermes **wins five whole days off the Moon at draughts** (Plutarch, Isis and Osiris 12); and Fortuna ran a **lot-drawing oracle** at Praeneste (Cicero, De Divinatione 2.85–86). The wager-frame requires no bending.

**Modern-life attribution (owner seed, canon §4):** Greek gods play the table in **Hawaii-vacation mode** — floral shirts, resort-wear, holiday self-importance; the Roman skins of the same identities are **food-obsessed** (Saturn treats his deposition buffet as a festival). Applied through the `temperament` lines; syncretic groups (zeus_jupiter, hades_pluto, etc.) are ONE playable identity each with the Roman variant as a skin/alias, per Wave-4 dedup policy.

**Selection (15 of a 40-candidate census pool):** every `luck_gambling` flag (Hermes, Tyche, Moirai) and every `forsaken_candidate` flag (Kronos, Pan, Prometheus) was extracted, plus mandatory Zeus, high-value patrons Athena and Hades, heroes Heracles and Orpheus, bosses Medusa and Sphinx, and content-seeds Sisyphus and Charon. Famous omissions (Poseidon, Apollo, Artemis, Aphrodite, Hera, Dionysus, Persephone, Hydra, Cerberus, Minotaur, Pandora's Pithos, Golden Fleece…) are deferred to a second Greek batch — the census depth note is right that this family will not run dry.

## 2. Source method

Ladder honored: public-domain primary translations (Perseus Digital Library, sacred-texts, Theoi's sourced text library) > academic reference > Wikipedia as index only. Web verification this session (2026-07-18) confirmed the five least-common load-bearing claims:

- Plutarch, *Isis and Osiris* 12 (Hermes wins the five epagomenal days from the Moon at draughts) — verified at penelope.uchicago.edu (Loeb text): "playing at draughts with the moon, he won from her the seventieth part of each of her periods of illumination… composed five days."
- Aesop, *The Goddess Fortune and the Man by the Well* — verified as Perry Index 174 / Babrius 49 (mythfolklore.net/aesopica Oxford translation, fable 470).
- Cicero, *De Divinatione* 2.85–86 (Numerius Suffustius and the sortes of Praeneste, cult-origin legend of Fortuna Primigenia) — verified via Perseus/Loeb.
- Aeschylus, *Eumenides* 723–728 (Apollo plied the Moirai with wine to reprieve Admetus) — verified via Perseus text with Smyth's note.
- Charon punished with a year in chains for ferrying the living Heracles (Servius on Aeneid 6.392; Roman-period tradition) — verified via the Charon literature survey (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charon, claim checked one level down to the Servius attribution).

Everything else rests on the standard PD primary corpus cited per entity below. No invented myths; every forward-declared `myth_greek_roman_*` id below is sourced.

## 3. Entities

### §Zeus (`greek_roman_zeus`) — FROZEN CALIBRATION ENTITY
**Scores:** influence 2 / recognition 5 / risk none / vip — exact match to frozen `greek_zeus`.
**Citations:** Homer, *Iliad* 15.187–193 (the three-way division of the cosmos **by lot** — Poseidon speaking: "all was divided among us three ways"); Hesiod, *Theogony* 453–506 (birth, the swallowed stone), 617–735 (Titanomachy); *Theogony* 775–806 (the binding oath by Styx); Pausanias 5.24.9 (Zeus Horkios, the oath-statue at Olympia where athletes swore); Ovid, *Met.* 8.611–724 (Baucis and Philemon — Jupiter tests guest-right); Ovid, *Met.* 1.163–252 (Lycaon punished for a corrupt feast).
**Personality rationale:** generosity 3 (grants kingships and favors, always transactionally); strictness 4 (oath- and guest-right-enforcer: Lycaon, Tantalus, Ixion all destroyed over hospitality/oath crimes); pettiness 3 (transforms inconvenient mortals; carries the Prometheus grudge for an age); wrath 4 (the thunderbolt résumé: Salmoneus, Asclepius); **fidelity 1 — the deal-sheet axis writes itself: mythology's most famous contract-breaker abandons consorts and champions alike, so his patronage is gloriously strong and structurally unreliable** (low fidelity = sells your contract, per §5 semantics); risk_appetite 3 (won the cosmos on a lot-draw but rules as a hedger — swallowed Metis specifically to dodge a succession bet).
**Myth leads (Wave 3):** `myth_greek_roman_division_of_cosmos` (world_myth — the casino's founding legend: the universe was a dice game); `myth_greek_roman_titanomachy` (world_myth).

### §Athena (`greek_roman_athena`)

**Personality rationale:** generosity 3 (gives tools and counsel: the olive, Bellerophon's bridle, Perseus's mirror-shield); strictness 4 (Arachne, Ajax the Lesser); pettiness 3 (the Arachne verdict and the Paris-judgment grudge are textbook rival-curse behavior); wrath 3 (punishes hard but not hair-trigger); fidelity 4 (stands by Odysseus, Perseus, Heracles across whole careers — the anti-Zeus on this axis); risk_appetite 2 (never gambles; wins Athens by preparing the better play).
**Myth leads:** `myth_greek_roman_birth_of_athena` (world_myth), `myth_greek_roman_contest_for_athens` (local_legend — and the patron side-bet template), `myth_greek_roman_arachne` (folk_tale/local_legend — contest, boast, punishment; premium taboo-demonstration material).

### §Hades (`greek_roman_hades`)

### §Hermes (`greek_roman_hermes`) — census flags: luck_gambling, messenger
**Citations:** *Homeric Hymn* 4 to Hermes (day one of life: invents the lyre from a tortoise, steals Apollo's cattle, perjures himself charmingly before Zeus, settles the suit by trading the lyre — the complete trickster-dealer CV, incl. the gift of the lot-oracle of the Thriae at 550–566); Plutarch, *Isis and Osiris* 12 (VERIFIED: "Hermes" playing draughts with the Moon wins the seventieth part of her light and builds the five extra days of the year — the god literally gambles new time into existence). **Honesty caveat:** the Plutarch story is interpretatio graeca of Egyptian Thoth — Plutarch himself names the player Hermes, so it is collected here with the syncretic caveat recorded; Wave 4 must cross-link, not duplicate, against any Egyptian Thoth entity. Windfalls: a lucky find was idiomatically a *hermaion* (Plato, *Gorgias* 486e; LSJ s.v.); heralds are sacrosanct under his office (*Iliad* 1.334).
**Personality rationale:** generosity 4 (the windfall god — hermaia are free gifts by definition); strictness 1 (rules are suggestions); pettiness 1 (no recorded grudges — laughs everything off); wrath 2 (Battus turned to stone is nearly the whole wrath file); fidelity 3 (everyone's courier — reliable, but working every side of the floor); risk_appetite 5 (committed grand theft on his birth-day; ≥4 = eager Forsaken host per §5, though his natural seat is dealer/messenger).
**Myth leads:** `myth_greek_roman_hermes_cattle_theft` (heroic_epic-grade hymn narrative), `myth_greek_roman_hermes_dice_with_the_moon` (world_myth — calendar-shaping wager; carries the Thoth caveat).

### §Tyche (`greek_roman_tyche`) — census flag: luck_gambling
**Citations:** Hesiod, *Theogony* 360 (Tyche the Oceanid); Pindar, *Olympian* 12 (invoked as "child of Zeus the Deliverer, saviour Tyche" — both genealogies recorded, dossier keeps Pindar's for the relations table); Aesop, Perry 174 / Babrius 49 (VERIFIED: Tyche and the man asleep at the well — "if you had fallen in, you would have blamed ME": the goddess's own statement of blame-taboo); Cicero, *De Divinatione* 2.85–86 (VERIFIED: the sortes of Praeneste — Fortuna Primigenia's lot-oracle and its flint-stone origin legend); Pliny, *Natural History* 2.5.22 (in his day "everywhere Fortuna alone is invoked" — the scale of Roman cult behind influence 2).

**Myth leads:** `myth_greek_roman_tyche_and_the_traveler` (folk_tale), `myth_greek_roman_sortes_of_praeneste` (local_legend — cult-origin story; in-game fortune-teller station seed).

### §Moirai (`greek_roman_moirai`) — census flag: luck_gambling · NOT patron_capable
**Citations:** Hesiod, *Theogony* 217–222 and 904–906 (double genealogy: daughters of Night, or of Zeus and Themis; named Clotho, Lachesis, Atropos); Aeschylus, *Eumenides* 723–728 (VERIFIED: Apollo "beguiled the Fates with wine" to reprieve Admetus — the ONE recorded exploit of the odds office, conditional on a substitute stake: someone must die in his place); Apollodorus 1.8.2 + Ovid, *Met.* 8.451–525 (Meleager: life bound to a burning log — an externalized, visible doom-object); Pausanias 5.15.5 (altar of the Moirai at Olympia — cult attestation behind influence 2); *Iliad* 24.209 (spinning fate at birth).

**Design ruling made here:** patron_capable **false** — they take no sides and back no contestants; they ARE the odds board. Their luck_gambling domain flag is honest at the office level (Lachesis = "drawer of lots"; kleromancy and fate were one office in Greek thought) — the house edge, not chance. The Admetus wine-exploit seeds a hidden once-per-campaign fate-renegotiation event priced in someone else's life.
**Myth leads:** `myth_greek_roman_fates_cheated_for_admetus` (heroic_epic — pairs with Heracles wrestling Death, same story-cycle), `myth_greek_roman_meleager_firebrand` (heroic_epic).

### §Pan (`greek_roman_pan`) — census flag: forsaken_candidate
**Citations:** *Homeric Hymn* 19 to Pan (son of Hermes; "Pan" because he delighted ALL the gods — the hymn's own etymology); Herodotus 6.105 (Pheidippides meets Pan on the road before Marathon; Pan asks why Athens neglects him though he is friendly and helpful — the neglected-god grievance in a primary source! — and Athens founds his cave cult, torch-race included, after the victory); Plutarch, *De defectu oraculorum* 17 (the sailor Thamus ordered to proclaim "Great Pan is dead" — the only god whose death antiquity itself announced); Ovid, *Met.* 1.689–712 (Syrinx — the pipes are a memorial to a chase that failed).
**Personality rationale:** generosity 3 (helpful when honored — Marathon is the receipt); strictness 1 (wild); pettiness 2 (spooks, doesn't scheme); wrath 2 (panic terror is instant and impersonal — disturb his midday rest and the fear is everyone's problem, then it's over); fidelity 3 (loyal to Arcadia and those who keep his rites); risk_appetite 4 (a certified-dead god playing for resurrection is structurally all-in; ≥4 Forsaken-host threshold met).
**Forsaken WHY (game_hooks basis):** the depiction-policy v2 thesis — a god's table presence is his modern perception — makes Pan's arc irresistible: publicly dead, culturally everywhere (panic, pan-pipes), staking his remaining existence on a comeback table whose outcome unlocks new stages either way.
**Myth leads:** `myth_greek_roman_pan_at_marathon` (local_legend — historical-legendary cult foundation), `myth_greek_roman_death_of_pan` (world_myth — a god's death proclaimed to the world), `myth_greek_roman_pan_and_syrinx` (folk_tale).

### §Sisyphus (`greek_roman_sisyphus`)
**Citations:** Homer, *Iliad* 6.152–154 ("craftiest of men"); *Odyssey* 11.593–600 (the boulder, eternally re-rolled — the punishment in the oldest source); the Thanatos-binding (Sisyphus chains Death; nobody on earth can die until Ares frees him) survives via Pherecydes fr. 119 in the Iliad scholia; Theognis 702–712 (the second cheat: persuading Persephone to release him "to scold his wife," then simply not coming back). Two successful death-cheats, both primary-attested.
**Personality rationale:** generosity 2 (a xenia-violator in the sources — part of why Zeus escalated); strictness 2; pettiness 3 (the feud with Salmoneus); wrath 2; fidelity 1 (cheated Death, Persephone, his own funeral instructions, and Zeus's privacy in one career); risk_appetite 5 (bet against Death twice and won twice; the boulder is the house's answer to a player it could not out-play, only out-last).
**D-4 note:** fully legendary king of Corinth — no historicity claim (unlike the Midas name-collision flagged in the census; Midas was skipped partly for that reason).
**Myth leads:** `myth_greek_roman_sisyphus_cheats_death` (heroic_epic — covering both cheats and the boulder).

### §Charon (`greek_roman_charon`)
**Citations:** Aristophanes, *Frogs* 180–270 (the comic ferry ride — the oldest extended portrait; fare haggling included); Virgil, *Aeneid* 6.298–416 (the grim ferryman; the unburied wait a hundred years on the shore — the exact-change/wait-a-Clock mechanic is straight from here); the obol custom is pan-Hellenic funerary practice (Lucian, *On Funerals* 10 mocks it in detail); Servius on *Aeneid* 6.392 (VERIFIED one level down: a year in chains for ferrying the living Heracles — the one forced exception, punished).
**Personality rationale:** generosity 1 (exact fare, no credit); strictness 5 (no coin, no crossing — centuries of queue); pettiness 2; wrath 2 (grumbles, complies, files the incident); fidelity 5 (the cosmos's only 100%-uptime service); risk_appetite 1 (the safest book in the underworld).
**Myth leads:** appears in `myth_greek_roman_orpheus_katabasis` (charmed by music — the one legitimate bribe) and `myth_greek_roman_twelve_labors` (the Cerberus labor — the one successful use of force, and its price).
