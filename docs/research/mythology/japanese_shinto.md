## Tradition overview and casino angle

Shinto is a **living majority practice** (80,000+ shrines; roughly half of Japan
participates in shrine practice without self-identifying as "Shintoist" — census record,
verified Wave 1), with a superb public-domain primary corpus: the **Kojiki** (712 CE,
Chamberlain tr. 1882, PD) and **Nihongi / Nihon Shoki** (720 CE, Aston tr. 1896, PD).
Source_quality 5 supports a full 15-entity extraction. Every venerated kami carries
`depiction_risk: living` — recorded as DATA per the 2026-07-18 depiction-policy v2 ruling
(pop-culture register; Shinto-themed anime is an owner-named precedent, so this tradition
is squarely inside the intended register).

**Casino frame.** This pantheon is astonishingly pre-adapted to the Cosmic Casino:

- The central myth of the tradition — **Ama-no-Iwato** — is a god sulking backstage while
  the world's broadcast goes dark, fixed by a bawdy stage act, a roaring audience, and a
  mirror: the first hype-meter comeback in world mythology.
- The **ukehi** vow between Amaterasu and Susanoo is a formal divine **wager** with
  declared stakes and adjudicated outcome.
- Susanoo is literally **fined "a thousand tables"** and expelled (Kojiki, Chamberlain's
  rendering — verified quote); the pantheon invented losing your tables.
- The **Seven Lucky Gods** are a branded luck-and-wealth franchise; **Zeniarai Benzaiten**
  devotees wash money in a cave spring so it multiplies; **Toka Ebisu** is a three-day
  luck market drawing over a million visitors.
- **Kamiarizuki**: once a year, all eight million kami attend a conference at Okuninushi's
  venue to settle fates and matches — an annual all-patrons shareholder meeting.

**Modern-life attribution (per canon §4, extend-per-pantheon):** the Shinto delegation
plays as a **family-run mega talent agency / TV network** — eight million contracted
"talents," an idol industry's obsession with purity scandals, ratings, and comeback
specials. Amaterasu is the chairwoman-emeritus who once cancelled all programming;
Susanoo the scandal-tour rock star; Inari runs franchising; Okuninushi is the bought-out
founder; the Lucky Seven are the beloved variety-show regulars. Used in every

## §Ebisu — the leech-child returned, luck flag

**Influence 3** — Nishinomiya Jinja heads ~3,500 Ebisu shrines; Toka Ebisu (Jan 9–11)

**Myth verified.** *Hiruko set adrift* (Kojiki; identification verified): the first
child of Izanagi and Izanami, born boneless ("leech-child") because the bride spoke
first at the wedding rite, set adrift in a reed boat. Medieval tradition (Muromachi —

to become the laughing god of fishers and merchants — the only one of the Seven Lucky
Gods native to Japan. The hard-of-hearing tradition is live practice: worshippers at
Nishinomiya clap loudly (and knock at the rear of the shrine) to get his attention —
verified; it is the direct source of the "announce your bets out loud" favor condition.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 4, wrath 1, pettiness 1, strictness 1 (the entirely
benign member of the roster — his punishments are mere absence of luck); fidelity 5
(the god who was abandoned and came back holding no grudge; merchants keep him for
life); risk_appetite 4 (patron of fishermen — the original windfall economy; he plays
for the joy of it).

**Forsaken resonance:** heaven's own discard who floated home — the cheap-seats proof

forsaken_host role; the resonance is texture, recorded here honestly.

**Lead:** `myth_japanese_shinto_hiruko_adrift` (world_myth fringe / local_legend at
