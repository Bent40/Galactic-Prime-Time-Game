# Mesoamerican tradition — Wave 2 extraction dossier

Researcher session: 2026-07-18 · Tradition: `mesoamerican` (Aztec/Mexica + Maya) ·
15 entities extracted (9 patron-capable gods · 1 hero · 1 fiend · 2 beasts · 1 spirit · 1 artifact).
Census pool: 40 candidates (Wave 1). Source-quality 4 per `traditions.json` — deep enough for a full batch,
with the caveat that Aztec-side narrative survives largely through colonial chroniclers (Sahagún's Florentine
Codex, the Leyenda de los Soles, Histoyre du Mechique) and the Maya side rests overwhelmingly on one
extraordinary primary, the Popol Vuh.

## Tradition overview — the casino angle

No tradition in the census is more natively casino-shaped. The Aztec cosmos literally runs on a
tribute-for-broadcast economy: the Fifth Sun only moves because gods and humans keep feeding it — spectacle
as infrastructure. The Aztecs had a dedicated god of gambling (Macuilxochitl) under whom players staked
homes, land, and their own freedom at patolli; the Maya underworld's ruling lords lure the living into
rigged ballgame wagers with life as the stake, go all-in against two trickster brothers, and are BANKRUPTED
— their worship formally revoked in the text. That is the §5 Forsaken amendment written down five centuries
early.

**Proposed pantheon-level modern-life attribution** (per canon §4 — owner seed examples pattern):
the **Aztec table** plays as the original extreme-spectacle producers — the people who invented
pay-per-view apocalypse, who know the sun is a subscription service and the show must be fed; the
**Maya table** plays as the casino's actuaries and oddsmakers — calendar quants who computed the
long-count odds tables before anyone else had a zero. Both flavors are woven into the temperament lines.

## Quetzalcoatl — god, patron (VIP)

**Myth basis (verified).** (1) *The bones of Mictlan* (Leyenda de los Soles): Quetzalcoatl descends to
Mictlan for the bones of the previous humanity; Mictlantecuhtli sets a rigged trial — sound a conch with no
holes — which Quetzalcoatl beats by having worms bore holes and bees hum inside; fleeing with the bones he is
ambushed by quail, falls into a pit, and the broken bones become differently-sized humans, revived when the
gods (Cihuacoatl grinding, Quetzalcoatl bleeding onto the meal) give their own blood. Verified via
wilderutopia.com, humanityarchived.com, en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mictlāntēcutli, worldhistory.org/Mictlantecuhtli.
(2) *The fall of Tollan* (Florentine Codex Book III, LOC digitization): Tezcatlipoca-Titlacauan shows him his
aged face in a mirror and tempts him with pulque; disgraced, he burns his palaces and departs east on a raft
of serpents, vowing return. (3) Co-creator of the earth from the primordial monster (Histoyre du Mechique —
see Cipactli). **Personality rationale.** Generosity 4: gave his own blood to make humanity, twice a
civilizational culture-bringer. Strictness 3: priestly ascetic standard. Pettiness 1: blamed himself, not
others, and left. Wrath 2: his catastrophes are grief-shaped, not vengeance. Fidelity 4: the vow of return is
the core of his myth. Risk 2: the one time he accepted an unexamined gamble (the drink) it ended his reign —
he learned. **Deal-sheet notes.** Favor conditions map to the bone-rescue (carry the fallen out), the
anti-sacrifice stance attributed to his Tollan cult (killless resolutions), priest-of-learning (solve before
fighting), and blood-gift (share when both are low). Taboos: executing the surrendered; the unexamined drink.
**Forsaken lead:** deposed god planning a comeback — flagged forsaken_host. **Myth records declared:**
`myth_mesoamerican_bones_of_mictlan`, `myth_mesoamerican_fall_of_tollan`, `myth_mesoamerican_earth_from_cipactli`.

## Tezcatlipoca — god, patron (VIP)

**Myth basis (verified).** The Florentine Codex paints him as the arbitrary fortune-god: Titlacauan, "We Are
His Slaves," who "brings wealth and misery," whose gifts end in ruin (encyclopedia.com/Tezcatlipoca;
nahuatl.wired-humanities.org). He ruins Quetzalcoatl with a mirror and a drink (FC Book III); he loses his
foot baiting the earth-monster at the start of the world (Wikipedia/Tlaltecuhtli, verified); he presides over
the First Sun era in the Five Suns cycle. Necoc Yaotl, "Enemy of Both Sides," is his attested epithet.
**Personality rationale.** Generosity 3: he genuinely showers fortune — before revoking it. Strictness 2: no
fixed law, only whim. Pettiness 5: destroyed a rival's whole civilization over precedence. Wrath 4:
punishment-as-theatre. Fidelity 1: Enemy of Both Sides — he sells everyone. Risk 5: bets his own body parts
(the foot) and delights in chaos — an eager Forsaken host per §5. **Deal-sheet notes.** luck_gambling is in
his domains deliberately: Sahagún's fortune-language makes him the tradition's god of the reversal — the
house's volatility itself. Taboo "never repeat a tactic" generates the most interesting player behavior in
the roster. **Myth records:** `myth_mesoamerican_fall_of_tollan`, `myth_mesoamerican_earth_from_cipactli`,
`myth_mesoamerican_five_suns`.

## Huitzilopochtli — god, patron (VIP)

**Myth basis (verified).** The birth at Coatepec (FC Book III, ch. 1 — LOC + hemi.nyu.edu text; davidbowles.us
translation): Coatlicue conceives by a feather-ball; her daughter Coyolxauhqui leads the Four Hundred
Southerners to kill her; Huitzilopochtli is born fully armed, takes up the xiuhcoatl fire-serpent, dismembers
Coyolxauhqui, and routs the four hundred. As solar war god he must be fed tribute to keep the sun moving.
**Personality rationale.** Generosity 2: his blessings are earned in blood, never given. Strictness 4:
tribute has a schedule. Pettiness 3: middling — his rages are causal, not petty, but slights to his mother
were answered with dismemberment. Wrath 5: the birth myth IS a wrath story. Fidelity 4: carried his people
through the migration to Tenochtitlan and never dropped them. Risk 3: aggressive but not a gambler — war
is his business model, not his vice. **Myth record:** `myth_mesoamerican_coatepec_birth`.

## Tlaloc — god, patron (VIP)

**Myth basis (verified).** Governs the Third Sun, which ends in a rain of fire after (in the common account)
Tezcatlipoca abducts his wife Xochiquetzal and grieving Tlaloc withholds rain, then answers the begging with
burning rain (historycooperative.org, storytellingdb.com — primary: Historia de los Mexicanos por sus
Pinturas / Leyenda de los Soles). The four jars / four rains — one beneficial, the others "fiery rain, fungus
rain, wind rain, flint blade rain" — are attested in the codical tradition (mexicolore.co.uk Tlaloc PDF;
Wikipedia/Tláloc, verified). His paradise Tlalocan receives the drowned and lightning-struck (FC Book III
appendix). **Personality rationale.** Generosity 3: the indispensable provider. Strictness 4: ritual
precision demanded. Pettiness 4: rained fire on the world over a personal grievance. Wrath 4: drought,
flood, lightning as escalation ladder. Fidelity 3: reliable while honored. Risk 2: a jar-keeper, not a
bettor. **Myth records:** `myth_mesoamerican_third_sun_fire_rain`, `myth_mesoamerican_five_suns`.

## Mictlantecuhtli — god, patron (VIP)

**Myth basis (verified).** The bones episode from the house's side (Leyenda de los Soles; worldhistory.org,
mythopedia.com, Wikipedia — all one-level-down checked): he agrees to hand over the bones, attaches the
holeless-conch condition, is out-cheated, pretends acceptance, then digs a pit and sends quail — procedure,
trap, and collections, in that order. Skeletal lord of the ninth layer, owl-and-spider iconography, married
co-ruler (Mictecacihuatl — censused, not extracted this batch). **Personality rationale.** Generosity 2:
everything is priced. Strictness 5: his house has RULES. Pettiness 4: rigged the conch rather than refuse
outright. Wrath 3: retaliation by protocol, not fury. Fidelity 3: honors the letter, never the spirit.
Risk 4: accepts wager-trials with souls at stake rather than simply saying no — he plays. **Deal-sheet
notes.** The resurrection-item taboo is the roster's most gameplay-legible taboo, and his at-death's-door
multiplier makes him the natural patron of glass-cannon runs. **Myth record:** `myth_mesoamerican_bones_of_mictlan`.

## Macuilxochitl — god, patron (Normal) — CASINO NOBILITY

**Myth basis (verified).** The dedicated Aztec god of gambling and games: patron of patolli and the ballgame;
a Codex Magliabechiano plate shows him presiding over a patolli game in progress; winnings were treated as
his gift; players invoked him on the throw. Durán (c. 1580) calls patolli the Aztecs' most common game and
with Sahagún records stakes escalating from copper bells and jade to homes, land, and the players' own
freedom — gambling into slavery (mexicolore.co.uk "Gambling and patolli, the Aztecs' favourite game" +
"Games and Other Amusements"; Wikipedia/Patolli, verified one level down). Sahagún (FC Book I) records his
feast and its fasting taboo: breakers were struck with embarrassing ailments (boils, hemorrhoids, genital
disease) — divine punishment as humiliation, not death. **Personality rationale.** Generosity 4: god of
pleasure, feast, and the winnings-as-gift economy. Strictness 2: one law — honor the game. Pettiness 2 /
Wrath 2: punishments are embarrassing, never lethal. Fidelity 2: follows the fun. Risk 5: the definitional
gambling god. **Casino notes.** Flag carried from census: luck_gambling — per spec §4 he is casino nobility.
An influence-1 pauper with risk-appetite 5 is also the amendment's Forsaken-host profile: the first god
who'd stake his own existence. **Honesty note.** His myth_ref (`myth_mesoamerican_five_flower_patolli`) is a
ritual-and-practice account (codex plate + chroniclers), not a narrative myth — Wave 3 should grade it
folk_tale/local_legend. Xochipilli overlap: many sources treat Macuilxochitl as a calendrical aspect of
Xochipilli; census lists both; Wave 4 should rule on merging.

## Xolotl — god, patron (Normal) — Forsaken-flagged

**Myth basis (verified).** Sahagún Book VII and the Leyenda de los Soles: when the newly-created Fifth Sun
demanded the gods' deaths to set it moving, Xolotl wept and fled his own sacrifice, transforming into a
double maize stalk (xolotl), a double maguey (mexolotl), and finally the water-creature axolotl — and was
caught and killed anyway (worldhistory.org/Xolotl; mexicolore.co.uk/axolotl; raco.cat academic article — all
converging on the same primary). Twin of Quetzalcoatl; evening star; dog-headed psychopomp who accompanies
his brother to Mictlan in the bones episode (wilderutopia.com account, Leyenda-based). **Personality
rationale.** Generosity 3: a service god — he carries, escorts, accompanies. Strictness 2, pettiness 1: no
grudges recorded. Wrath 1: he is myth's victim, not its punisher. Fidelity 5: the loyal dog of the dead, the
brother who went down into Mictlan too. Risk 1: fled the ultimate stake — the roster's risk floor.
**Forsaken lead (census flag).** A god who was UNMADE on the record. Hosting, for him, is desperation — the
amendment's tragic case, deliberately opposite to Macuilxochitl's appetite case. **Myth records:**
`myth_mesoamerican_flight_of_xolotl`, `myth_mesoamerican_bones_of_mictlan`, `myth_mesoamerican_fifth_sun_teotihuacan`.

## Nanahuatzin — god, patron (Normal) — THE Forsaken exemplar

**Myth basis (verified).** The creation of the Fifth Sun at Teotihuacan (Sahagún Book VII; Leyenda de los
Soles; corroborated across the Xolotl verification sources, which retell the same episode): the gods gather
in darkness to make a new sun; rich Tecciztecatl offers quetzal feathers and gold where poor, pustuled
Nanahuatzin offers reeds, maguey spines, and his own scabs; at the bonfire Tecciztecatl balks four times;
Nanahuatzin shuts his eyes and jumps, becoming the Sun (Tecciztecatl, jumping second into the cooled ash,
becomes the Moon). An eagle follows him into the flames (charred wing-tips); the new sun then refuses to
move until the assembled gods yield their own blood. **Personality rationale.** Generosity 4: gave his body
for the world. Strictness 3: one standard — total commitment. Pettiness 1: none recorded. Wrath 2: meek
pre-transformation; the sun's tribute-demand keeps this above 1. Fidelity 5: did not flinch. Risk 5: the
purest all-in in world mythology. **Casino notes.** The §5 amendment asks for gods with nothing staking
everything: this is that myth, verbatim — a nothing god goes all-in with his own existence and wins the
biggest pot on the board. Also the divine mirror of the canon's mortal-to-god pipeline. Wave-4 flag:
Tonatiuh (censused separately) is what Nanahuatzin becomes; recommend one entity, not two. **Myth records:**
`myth_mesoamerican_fifth_sun_teotihuacan`, `myth_mesoamerican_five_suns`.

## Huracan — god, patron (VIP) — Maya, depiction_risk: living

**Myth basis (verified).** Popol Vuh (Christenson/Mesoweb; Goetz–Morley text at latinamericanstudies.org;
summary corroboration at mayan.org and bibliotecapleyades.net): Heart of Sky — the one-legged storm triad
(Thunderbolt Huracan, Newborn Thunderbolt, Sudden Thunderbolt) — speaks creation into being with Sovereign
Plumed Serpent; the mud people fail and dissolve; the wooden people fail to remember their makers and are
annihilated by flood and resin-rain while their own tools, dogs, and grinding-stones turn on them (and
Camazotz, as Camalotz, snatches off their heads); true humans are finally shaped from maize. He also
directs the Hero Twins against the arrogant false sun Seven Macaw. **Depiction note.** Census flag `living`
carried: contemporary K'iche' daykeepers still name Huracan in prayer (Wave-1 verification, Smithsonian
"Living Maya Time"). Policy v2: recorded as data, no gate; portray respectfully. **Personality rationale.**
Generosity 3: creation itself, but earned. Strictness 4: scrapped two humanities for falling short.
Pettiness 3: the wooden people's capital crime was ingratitude. Wrath 5: world-flood tier. Fidelity 4: kept
iterating until humanity worked, then kept them. Risk 3: a builder who will scrap and rebuild — measured,
not reckless. **Myth records:** `myth_mesoamerican_popol_vuh_creation`, `myth_mesoamerican_wooden_people_flood`.

## Hunahpu and Xbalanque (the Hero Twins) — hero (Normal)

**Myth basis (verified).** Popol Vuh, Parts II–IV (Christenson; ebsco.com Research Starters; Wikipedia/Maya
Hero Twins — checked one level down against the text): summoned to Xibalba like their father before them,
the twins survive the trial houses (Dark, Razor, Cold, Jaguar, Bat), send a mosquito ahead to sting each
lord so the others cry out his name — learning the lords' true names and exposing the mannequin decoys;
Hunahpu is decapitated by Camazotz and a carved squash substitutes for his head; they let themselves be
killed, have their ground bones thrown in the river, return disguised as vagabond performers whose act is
sacrificing and reviving each other — until One Death and Seven Death beg "Do it to us! Sacrifice us!"
(Tedlock's rendering), and the twins oblige, permanently. They rise as sun and moon. They also blowgun the
false sun Seven Macaw from his perch (Part I). **Personality rationale.** Generosity 3; strictness 2 (rule
one: the con must land); pettiness 3: Xibalba's humiliation is total and forever — they avenge, thoroughly;
wrath 3; fidelity 5: each other, absolute, through decapitation and back; risk 5: staked their lives on a
resurrection trick working. **Casino notes.** contestant_legend: the archetypal players who beat the house.
Their myth chain is the epithet-template motherlode (Wave 3: deed_profile heavy on mind + charm — the
ballgame is reflexes, the con is charm). **Myth records:** `myth_mesoamerican_xibalba_ballgame`,
`myth_mesoamerican_fall_of_seven_macaw`.

## One Death and Seven Death — fiend (Normal) — luck_gambling + Forsaken flags

**Myth basis (verified).** Popol Vuh: chief lords of Xibalba who lure ballplayers down with rigged
challenges — the hot-stone bench, the unsmokable cigar that must stay lit all night, the White Dagger ball
with a hidden blade — having previously defeated and sacrificed the twins' father Hun Hunahpu on exactly
these terms. Their end: enchanted by the twins' resurrection act, they demand to be sacrificed themselves,
and are; their surviving subjects are stripped of worship — the text explicitly demotes Xibalba to receiving
only worthless offerings thereafter. **Personality rationale.** Generosity 1; strictness 5 (their game,
their rules, every rule a trap); pettiness 5 (they murder guests for losing at hospitality); wrath 4;
fidelity 1; risk 5 — they went all-in against the twins and lost the house. **Casino notes.** The census's
luck_gambling + forsaken_candidate fiend: they ARE the casino's cautionary tale — hosts who staked
everything on a rigged table and were formally BANKRUPTED (worship revoked = the spec's god-bankruptcy,
in the primary text). Boss design in the entity record. **Myth record:** `myth_mesoamerican_xibalba_ballgame`.

## Camazotz — beast (Normal)

**Myth basis (verified).** Popol Vuh: the snatch-bat of the Bat House who decapitates Hunahpu the instant he
peeks out of his blowgun; identified with Camalotz, the head-taker among the wooden people's destroyers
(bibliotecapleyades.net PV text; mayan.org summary). Modern recognition sustained by videogame presence
(Smite's playable Camazotz) and steady pop-myth circulation. **Personality rationale** (beast register):
generosity 1; strictness 3 (one law: the dark is his); pettiness 1 (nothing personal); wrath 4; fidelity 2
(serves Xibalba's houses when it suits); risk 3. Enemy sketch in the entity record — bleeding affinity,
darkness stillness-phases, squash-decoy win condition. **Myth records:** `myth_mesoamerican_xibalba_ballgame`,
`myth_mesoamerican_wooden_people_flood`.

## Cipactli — beast (Normal)

**Myth basis (verified, with an honesty flag).** The primordial crocodilian sea-monster from whom the earth
was made: Quetzalcoatl and Tezcatlipoca descend as serpents, Tezcatlipoca offers his foot as bait and loses
it to her bite, and the two tear her in two — sky from one half, earth from the other; her body remains
alive and hungry, demanding hearts (Wikipedia/Tlaltecuhtli and /Cipactli, verified; worldhistory.org/Tlaltecuhtli;
primary: Histoyre du Mechique). **Conflation flag:** the tearing myth's monster is named Tlaltecuhtli in the
Histoyre du Mechique; Cipactli is the primordial caiman of related cosmogonies and the first day-sign; many
sources merge them. Shipped as Cipactli with Tlaltecuhtli recorded as a variant — Wave 4 may split into two
records if the earth-lord's separate iconography is wanted. **Personality:** pure hunger — wrath 5,
everything else floor values; risk 2 (she took the bait). Colossal terrain-boss sketch in the entity record
(crushed/suffocation affinities; bait-a-body-part discoverable win — direct synergy with GPT's body-part HP).
**Myth record:** `myth_mesoamerican_earth_from_cipactli`.

## Cihuateteo — spirit (Normal)

**Myth basis (verified).** Sahagún (FC Books I and IV) and the codical corpus: women who died in first
childbirth were honored as fallen warriors (mocihuaquetzque) — childbirth was battle; as the Cihuateteo,
they descend on five day-signs (1 Deer, 1 Rain, 1 Monkey, 1 House, 1 Eagle) to haunt crossroads, where
shrines received offerings; they strike children with seizure and wasting sickness and travelers with
madness; they escort the sun from zenith to sunset (Wikipedia/Cihuateteo; azteccalendar.com/god/Cihuateteo;
Met Museum object commentary; womenandmyth.org — cross-checked). The La Llorona connection is popularly
drawn via Cihuacoatl; noted, not claimed. **Personality rationale:** generosity 2 (they can bless — they
were honored, not damned); strictness 3 (calendar-bound, shrine-appeasable); pettiness 3 (they take small
things — they reach for what they lost); wrath 4 (the sickness record); fidelity 4 (eternal escort duty,
kept); risk 2. Event/NPC seeds in the entity record. **Myth record:** `myth_mesoamerican_cihuateteo_descent`.

## Xiuhcoatl — artifact (Normal)

**Myth basis (verified).** The turquoise fire-serpent — the weapon Huitzilopochtli takes up at his birth on
Coatepec to dismember Coyolxauhqui and rout the Four Hundred (FC Book III ch. 1, hemi.nyu.edu text;
encyclopedia.com/Huitzilopochtli: "he took his xiuhcoatl, 'serpent of lightning,' and slaughtered the
attacking siblings"). In wider Aztec religion the xiuhcoatl is the sun's dart and the fire-god's nahual;
a famous stone example is in the British Museum corpus (Miller & Taube). **Record convention:** personality
axes model the relic as a semi-sentient weapon (fidelity 5 to its wielder, wrath 5 when loosed, strictness 4
in its feeding demands). God-Relic item sketch in the entity record — burn affinity, spectacle-charged,
dismember-sweep, punishes a cold wielder. **Myth record:** `myth_mesoamerican_coatepec_birth`.

## Selection notes — what was skipped and why

- **Kukulkan / Q'uq'umatz**: folded into Quetzalcoatl's `syncretic_group: feathered_serpent` as variants
  (Wave-4 to confirm the one-playable-identity rule); this freed a slot without losing the Maya serpent.
- **Tonatiuh**: skipped as a separate entity — he is what Nanahuatzin becomes; Wave-4 ruling requested.
- **Chaac**: strong candidate (living Ch'a Cháak practice) but overlaps Huracan on sky_storm and lost the
  Maya-patron slot to the better-sourced Popol Vuh corpus; recommend for any Wave-2b top-up.
- **Ixchel, Itzamna, Xochiquetzal, Coatlicue, Cihuacoatl, Itzpapalotl, Tonatiuh, Ehecatl, Paynal,
  Mictecacihuatl**: slot budget; all remain good future extractions. Mictecacihuatl additionally sits near
  the Santa Muerte continuity claim the census explicitly excludes — extract with care later.
- **Xipe Totec**: deliberately deferred — the flaying complex needs an owner taste ruling under the
  tradition's human-sacrifice-imagery caution before he gets a patron sheet.
- **Ixtab**: skipped on honesty grounds — modern scholarship doubts Landa's 'suicide goddess' framing;
  too thin to score.
- **Maximón, Alux, Xtabay**: census-flagged restricted/living (cofradía-held and community-held material);
  left for the owner-review lane rather than a 15-slot extraction batch.
- **Vucub-Caquix, Zipacna, Cabrakan, Tzitzimime, Ahuitzotl**: good bosses, lost slots to the
  luck_gambling/forsaken-flagged picks; Tzitzimime pair naturally with a future eclipse mechanic.
