# Polynesian & Māori Mythology — Wave-2 Extraction Dossier

Tradition: `polynesian_maori` · Extracted 2026-07-18 · 15 entities (7 patron-capable gods, 2 heroes, 3 beasts/fiends, 3 folk/spirit/artifact)

## Tradition overview — and the casino angle

The Polynesian triangle (Aotearoa, Hawaiʻi, Tahiti, Samoa, Tonga, Cook Islands, Rapa Nui) shares one ancestral pantheon refracted across archipelagos: Tangaroa/Kanaloa/Taʻaroa/Tagaloa, Tāne/Kāne, Rongo/Lono, Tū/Kū, Māui everywhere. Cognate identity is philologically established (Tregear, *Maori-Polynesian Comparative Dictionary*, 1891, PD) — this dossier treats each cognate set as ONE playable identity with archipelago skins, recorded via `syncretic_group` for Wave-4 to confirm.

**These are living indigenous cultures, not museum shelves.** Pele receives documented present-day offerings at Kīlauea (2018 eruption coverage: Honolulu Star-Advertiser 2018-05-13; The Wild Hunt 2018-05; hula hālau hoʻokupu before Merrie Monarch). Māori atua are invoked in living karakia; the NZ Army is formally *Ngāti Tūmatauenga*, the tribe of the war god (RNZAOC; Auckland War Memorial Museum). The census records a public backlash history against careless game use of Māori material (H1Z1 'Maui', 2019) — **every Māori/Hawaiian asset here should be treated as consultation-recommended**, and `depiction_risk` is recorded per entity as data under the 2026-07-18 depiction-policy v2 (living figures ARE rosterable; the bar is respectful-never-racist).

**Casino frame.** This pantheon's modern-life attribution writes itself as the inversion of the owner's Greek gag (Greeks play Hawaii-vacation mode): the Polynesian table are **the family that actually owns the waterfront everyone else vacations on** — the casino's original navigators, oldest voyaging club, first landlords. Deep bench of Forsaken material: Mahuika (bankrupted by one hustler, one stake left), Whiro (lost the knowledge bid, banished, plotting a comeback), Māui (mortal-side all-in against death, lost), and Hine-nui-te-pō (the only host with a mythologically proven undefeated record — the house that won a legend's all-in). Lono carries the tradition's `luck_gambling` flag: Makahiki was a real season of games and heavy wagering.

## Pantheon gods (patron-capable)

### Pele — volcano goddess (Hawaiʻi)

**Citations.** Migration from Kahiki, Halemaʻumaʻu home, Hiʻiaka cycle: Emerson 1915 (PD); Westervelt 1916 (PD); Beckwith 1940 ch. on Pele. Kamapuaʻa battle and the windward/leeward division of the islands: nps.gov/articles/kamapuaa.htm; lovebigisland.com/big-island-mythology/pele-kamapuaa (verified this session — Pele calls on her fire-keeper uncle Lonomakua; Kamapuaʻa answers with rain; stalemate divides wet Koʻolau from dry Kona). Living veneration: Star-Advertiser 2018-05-13 ("Many accept their fate at the hands of Pele"); The Wild Hunt 2018-05 (hoʻokupu as lava flows); Nimmo, *The Cult of Pele in Traditional Hawaiʻi* (Bishop Museum); hula hālau offerings before Merrie Monarch.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 3 — rewards hospitality lavishly in the disguised-wanderer tales, but gives on her terms. Strictness 4 — kapu around her ʻōhelo berries, offerings, and rocks ('Pele's curse' folk practice persists today). Pettiness 4 — the Kamapuaʻa and Poliʻahu feuds are grudges prosecuted across whole islands. Wrath 5 — she buries villages for slights (Kahawali cycle); 2018 commentary frames destruction as her prerogative. Fidelity 3 — passionate, possessive, and volatile in equal measure (she destroys her own beloved Lohiʻau in the Hiʻiaka cycle before restoring him). Risk 4 — she personally races chiefs downhill on hōlua sleds and trades chant-taunts before brawls; an eager, showy gambler.

**Myth leads (Wave 3):** `myth_polynesian_maori_pele_and_kamapuaa` (heroic_epic; stalemate → territorial division — reenactment: fight a rival to a draw and split the map on camera); `myth_polynesian_maori_pele_and_hiiaka` (heroic_epic; the errand to fetch Lohiʻau, the broken promise, the burned groves).

### Tangaroa — the ocean (pan-Polynesian: Kanaloa / Taʻaroa / Tagaloa)

**Citations.** Grey 1855, "The Children of Heaven and Earth": in the war after the separation, Tangaroa's grandchildren divide — fish flee to the sea, reptiles to the forest — and Tangaroa's feud with Tāne (who shelters the runaways and supplies canoes and hooks to fishermen) is explicit and eternal. Cognates: Tregear 1891 (Tangaroa/Kanaloa/Taʻaroa/Tagaloa); Taʻaroa world-shell cosmogony (Tahiti) and Tagaloa plover-and-maggots creation (Samoa) held at the census level. Living practice: karakia to Tangaroa in sea contexts is current mātauranga (Te Ara).

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 3 — the sea feeds everyone who keeps its rites. Strictness 4 — first-fish offerings and rāhui (closure) protocol are real, formal practice. Pettiness 4 — the fish-custody grievance against Tāne is mythology's longest-running sibling lawsuit. Wrath 4 — storms and swallowed canoes are his standard collections process. Fidelity 3 — tides commit to nothing. Risk 3 — vast but methodical; he plays position, not longshots.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_rangi_papa_separation` (world_myth; participant).

### Tāne — forests, birds, knowledge (Māori; Kāne in Hawaiʻi)

**Citations.** Separation of Rangi and Papa by Tāne's legs: Grey 1855. Three baskets of knowledge fetched from the summit of the heavens against Whiro's opposition: Best (NZETC, *Some Aspects of Maori Myth and Religion*); Te Ara; the story is NZ-curriculum standard (public-sphere — e.g. knowledge-basket.co.nz legend page). Note honestly: the baskets narrative descends through whare-wānanga recensions (Smith, *Lore of the Whare-wānanga*, 1913) whose esoteric layer (Io material) the census reports as closed — the PUBLIC baskets/climb story is extracted; the Io theology is not. Kāne identity: Tregear; Malo (Kāne as chief akua, no human sacrifice on his altars — census line). Rātā's tree re-erected by the forest folk until proper rites: Grey 1855 ("The Legend of Rata").

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 4 — separated the sky to give everything room, made the first woman, brought knowledge down for humankind. Strictness 3 — forest protocol enforced (Rātā), but he forgives once rites are done. Pettiness 2 — mostly a builder. Wrath 2 — his revenges are structural (giving canoes to fishermen), not tantrums. Fidelity 4 — climbed to the summit twice; finishes what he starts. Risk 3 — calculated daring: the ascent was audacious but prepared.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_rangi_papa_separation` (world_myth); `myth_polynesian_maori_tane_and_the_three_baskets` (world_myth; reenactment: recover three intel caches under harassment and win using them).

### Hine-nui-te-pō — death and night (Māori)

**Citations.** Grey 1855, "The Legend of Maui": Māui attempts to pass through her body to win immortality for humankind while she sleeps; the fantail's laugh wakes her; she crushes him — "the first man to die." Her flight to the underworld in shame (she was Hine-tītama, dawn maiden, who discovered her husband Tāne was her father) and her role receiving souls: Grey; Te Ara. Her iconography (obsidian teeth, greenstone-flashing eyes, sea-kelp hair) is from Grey's description.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 2 — she gives one thing only: a place for the dead. Strictness 5 — the single absolute law of the setting; no exceptions were made even for its greatest hero. Pettiness 1 — she pursues no one; you come to her. Wrath 4 — one strike, total, then calm; she does not escalate because she does not need to. Fidelity 5 — eternally where she said she would be. Risk 1 — the only figure at the casino who has never needed to gamble; the sure thing personified.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_death_of_maui` (world_myth; she is the house side of the setting's most famous all-in).

### Tūmatauenga — war and human endeavor (Māori; Kū in Hawaiʻi)

**Citations.** Grey 1855, "The Children of Heaven and Earth": alone among the brothers, Tū stands against Tāwhirimātea's storm assault and is not broken; he then takes utu on his brothers for abandoning him — snaring Tāne's birds, netting Tangaroa's fish, digging up Rongo's and Haumia's children — eating them and making them noa (common). His war-name list (Tū-kā-riri, Tū-kai-taua, etc.) is Grey's. Humanity descends from/aligns with Tū — hence war-rites route through him. Living invocation: the NZ Army is formally **Ngāti Tūmatauenga** (est. 1994; name gifted by Sir Charles Bennett of 28 Māori Battalion — rnzaoc.com; aucklandmuseum.com), with its own marae and haka. Kū identity + feather-god imagery and human-sacrifice temples: census line (Malo; Beckwith).

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 2 — spoils go to those who stood. Strictness 4 — war under him is ritual discipline, not brawling. Pettiness 3 — the brother-eating was justice by his lights, but he keeps score forever. Wrath 5 — his one recorded grievance ended with him consuming his brothers' children. Fidelity 4 — the god who did not run; he honors those who don't. Risk 4 — he chose to stand alone against a hurricane god; odds don't move him.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_rangi_papa_separation` (world_myth; his stand and utu — reenactment: win outnumbered without yielding ground).

### Lono — harvest, rain, games, healing (Hawaiʻi; Rongo across Polynesia) — LUCK_GAMBLING FLAG

**Citations.** Makahiki: Malo, *Hawaiian Antiquities* ch. 36 (Emerson tr. 1903, PD) — four months of the year when war was kapu, taxes collected, and the islands turned to boxing (mokomoko), wrestling, hōlua sledding, and surfing under the akua loa (Lonomakua image: crossbar pole, white kapa banner); betting on champions was standard, "with stakes of food or other valuables, and some daring souls even betting their lives" (Kaʻiwakīloumoku/KSBE essay "Makahiki: Let the Games Begin", verified this session; Webber's 1778 boxing-match drawing shows the Lono image presiding). Kaikilani: Beckwith 1940 ch. III (sacred-texts.com/pac/hm/hm05.htm) — Lono kills his wife in jealous error, travels the islands like a madman challenging all comers, institutes the games in her memory, departs promising return by sea (the myth into which Cook sailed; Cook-as-person stays OUT per D-4 — the episode is context only). Healing: the Lonopūhā healing tradition (Beckwith). Living revival: Makahiki ceremonies on Kahoʻolawe annually since 1982, calling Lono to bring rain (Protect Kahoʻolawe ʻOhana; hawaiipublicradio.org 2022-10-13 and 2023-12-14; Aluli v. Brown access ruling 1980). Rongo/Roʻo/Ono cognates: Tregear; Wikipedia-index verified.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 4 — the god of the season when everything is given away and rained on. Strictness 2 — loosest deal sheet on the roster; his ONE hard rule is the truce. Pettiness 2 — no recorded rivalry-prosecution. Wrath 3 — one catastrophic jealous outburst defines his whole myth; rare but real. Fidelity 3 — he departs and returns with the seasons; a cyclical partner. Risk 4 — patron of an entire institutionalized wagering season; the games are his liturgy.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_lono_and_kaikilani` (heroic_epic; grief → games — reenactment: after losing an ally, win a formal contest dedicated on camera to them).

### Mahuika — fire (Māori; pan-Polynesian Mahuike/Mafuiʻe) — FORSAKEN CANDIDATE

**Citations.** Grey 1855, "The Legend of Maui": Māui, pretending his fire keeps going out, begs his ancestress Mahuika for fire; she pulls out a burning fingernail; he drowns it and returns, again and again, until she has torn out nine and, realizing the con, hurls the last fire after him — the world burns, Māui barely escapes as a hawk, rain saves him, and the seeds of fire take refuge in the kaikōmako and other trees (why fire is drilled from wood). Pan-Polynesian variants (Mafuiʻe's earthquake-fire in Samoa; Mahuike below in Mangaia): Tregear; Westervelt, *Legends of Ma-ui*.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 4 — gave nine-tenths of her existence to a grandchild who asked nicely. Strictness 2 — she had one rule and didn't enforce it until too late. Pettiness 3 — she remembers exactly who did this to her. Wrath 4 — her response to ruin was attempted global arson. Fidelity 4 — kept honoring the kinship claim long past sense. Risk 5 — the roster's purest all-in profile: she staked everything, lost, and her surviving myth is the act of throwing her last asset after the loss. **Forsaken hosting per the §5 amendment: flagged as a god with nothing left who will stake her own existence — and as bankrupt-god casino staff (canon §4), she deals.**

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_maui_and_mahuika` (heroic_epic; the con and the conflagration).

## Heroes

### Māui — the trickster demigod (pan-Polynesian) — FORSAKEN CANDIDATE (mortal side)

**Citations.** Grey 1855 "The Legend of Maui" (Māori canonical arc): jawbone of Muri-ranga-whenua as weapon; snaring and beating Tama-nui-te-rā so the sun crawls; fishing up Te Ika-a-Māui (the North Island) with the jawbone hook baited with his own blood after his brothers refuse him bait; the Mahuika con; death inside Hine-nui-te-pō when the fantail laughs. Hawaiian cycle: Westervelt, *Legends of Ma-ui* (1910, PD). Pan-Polynesian identity: Tregear; EBSCO "Myth of Māui" index hit. Recognition: Disney's *Moana* (2016) made "Maui" a global household name — the FIGURE is traditional, Disney's design/voice is `modern_ip` to avoid in art direction (also the H1Z1 'Maui' backlash precedent: careless use of this exact figure has already burned a game publicly).

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 3 — his heists (fire, slower sun, new land) were for everyone, but he enjoyed the applause. Strictness 1 — rules are raw material. Pettiness 3 — turned his brother-in-law Irawaru into a dog over a fishing slight (Grey). Wrath 2 — tricks, not rages. Fidelity 2 — conned his own ancestress; cut his brothers out of the catch. Risk 5 — every famous act is an escalating dare, ending with betting his life against death itself and losing to a giggle.

**Myth leads:** four declared (sun-snare, land-fishing, Mahuika, death) — the densest single reenactment package in the tradition; his hooks are in the entity record.

### Tāwhaki — the lightning climber (Māori; Kahaʻi/Tafaʻi)

**Citations.** Grey 1855, "The Legend of Tawhaki": the Ponaturi sea-goblins murder his father Hema and enslave his mother; Tāwhaki and brother Karihi seal the goblins' sleeping-house and hold the door until dawn — sunlight kills the host wholesale. His ascent: at the hanging vines, Karihi grabs a loose aka and is swept away; Tāwhaki climbs by the fast root, obeying every instruction, reaches the heavens, and (in Grey's telling) wins back his celestial wife Tangotango and daughter Arahuta; lightning flashes from his armpits. Cognates Kahaʻi (Hawaiʻi)/Tafaʻi (Tahiti): Tregear; Beckwith.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 2 — a family avenger, not a public benefactor. Strictness 4 — his defining trait: exact ritual compliance is WHY he lives and Karihi doesn't. Pettiness 3 — grudges are his fuel (the whole Ponaturi genocide is one). Wrath 4 — annihilated a species for his father. Fidelity 4 — climbed heaven for his wife; freed his mother. Risk 4 — took the climb that had just killed his brother.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_tawhaki_and_the_ponaturi` (heroic_epic); `myth_polynesian_maori_tawhaki_climbs_to_heaven` (heroic_epic).

## Beasts & fiends

### Whiro — lord of darkness (Māori) — FORSAKEN CANDIDATE

**Citations.** Best (NZETC, *Some Aspects of Maori Myth and Religion*; *Maori Religion and Mythology*): Whiro-te-tipua opposes Tāne's ascent for the baskets, is defeated, descends to the underworld (Rarohenga/Te Pō), and from there wages permanent war on life; his emissaries the **Maiki brethren** are the personified diseases; lizards (ngārara, tuatara) are tapu-feared as his earthly manifestations (Te Ara "Ngārara – reptiles", verified this session). Whiro also names the dark night of the Māori lunar calendar.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 1 — sends only afflictions. Strictness 3 — his malice is systematic, not lawful. Pettiness 5 — cosmic-scale grudge maintained since the second-ever contest. Wrath 5 — his response to losing is a forever-war of plagues. Fidelity 2 — dark bargains, no loyalty. Risk 4 — he went all-in against Tāne once and would again; flagged forsaken_host: a deposed god wagering from the basement toward the day he devours the world.

**Myth leads:** `myth_polynesian_maori_tane_and_the_three_baskets` (world_myth; antagonist side). Boss sketch in the entity record (three-light-caches win condition; infected/poison/dissolution kit).

### Ponaturi — the sea-goblins (Māori)

**Citations.** Grey 1855, "The Legend of Tawhaki": pale goblin folk who live beneath the sea by day and sleep ashore in their house Manawa-Tāne by night; sunlight is fatal to them; exterminated by Tāwhaki and Karihi (with the captive mother Urutonga's guidance) via the propped-door dawn trick. Also appear in the Rātā cycle handling Wahieroa's bones.

**Axis rationale.** Pack profile: generosity 1, strictness 2 (mob logic), pettiness 3 (they keep trophies and slaves), wrath 4 (murdered Hema for trespass), fidelity 3 (die as a house), risk 2 — mythically defined by terror of one thing (dawn), the roster's most risk-averse entry. Enemy sketch in the entity record.

### Pouākai — the man-eating eagle (Māori, South Island)

**Citations.** NZ Geographic "Terror of the forest"; Ngāi Tahu educational resource "Te Pouākai" (orakaaparimarunaka.co.nz PDF, verified this session): the bird carries off men, women, children to its eyrie; the warrior **Te Hau-o-Tawera** and fifty men build a manuka-sapling trap by night; he baits the dive himself and the warriors spear the entangled bird. Widely linked in scholarship to the real extinct **Haast's eagle** (largest known eagle; moa predator; overlapped with Māori settlement). Ngāi Tahu treat pouākai narratives as taonga — depiction_risk low, consultation-recommended.

**Axis rationale.** An apex predator, not a person: wrath 4 (relentless raiding), fidelity 3 (fixed eyrie and hunting grounds — exploitable), risk 3 (bold stoops but wary of the ground), rest at floor. Boss sketch (trap/decoy win condition) in the entity record.

## Folk, spirit, artifact

### Menehune — the hidden builders (Hawaiʻi)

**Citations.** Beckwith 1940 ch. 23 (Menehune); the **Alekoko / Menehune Fishpond** legend (Kauaʻi): a 900-ft wall built in one night by workers passing stone hand to hand from Makaweli, abandoned if observed or unfinished by dawn (to-hawaii.com legend page; Wikipedia-index verified; the physical fishpond is a real registered historic site). Payment in a single shrimp per worker is standard in the tale corpus. Tahitian antecedent *manahune* (commoner class) per Tregear/Beckwith.

**Axis rationale.** Generosity 3 (they build wonders for shrimp), strictness 5 (one night, no watching, no exceptions — the entire myth is a labor contract), pettiness 2, wrath 2 (they don't punish, they LEAVE), fidelity 4 (work always delivered if terms are kept), risk 2. Event seed (Night Works + camera-visibility twist) in the entity record.

### Manaiakalani — Māui's fishhook (Hawaiʻi / pan-Polynesian)

**Citations.** Westervelt, *Legends of Ma-ui* (1910): the great hook Manaiakalani with which Māui fished up land; in Grey's Māori telling the hook is pointed with the jawbone of Muri-ranga-whenua and baited with Māui's own blood. Living star knowledge: **Manaiakalani ("The Chief's Fishline") is one of the four Hawaiian star lines** in modern Hōkūleʻa wayfinding — Ka Makau Nui o Māui (Scorpius) plus the Navigator's Triangle (archive.hokulea.com/ike/hookele/hawaiian_star_lines.html, verified this session; Nainoa Thompson's system). Item seed (blood-baited terrain-raising God Relic) in the entity record. Recognition rides Moana's "magic fishhook" image; Disney's specific design is protected.

### Huakaʻi pō — the Night Marchers (Hawaiʻi) — RESTRICTED (data flag)

**Citations.** Mary Kawena Pukui's 1930 account is the standard published source (via UH Mānoa Hawaiian Pacific Collection nightmarchers bibliography, www2.hawaii.edu/~hawnpac/libguides_docs/nightmarchers.pdf); Ka Wai Ola "Nā Huakaʻi o ka Pō" (kawaiola.news, verified this session): drums, torches, foul wind; look upon them and die; survival protocol is prostration, averted eyes, or calling one's moʻokūʻauhau (genealogy) in hope an ancestor in the ranks speaks for you. Belief is current in Hawaiʻi. Census flagged restricted; carried as data — the material is public-sphere published, the PROTOCOL is living belief; owner review recommended before shipping the event.

**Axis rationale.** A law, not a character: strictness 5, wrath 5 (instant, total), fidelity 5 (the route never changes), pettiness 1, generosity 1, risk 1. Event seed (procession + prostration + ancestor-pass) in the entity record; the ancestor-pass rule is a lovely hook into contestant-background mechanics.

## Candidates screened out (and why)

- **Kupe** (census-flagged restricted): great navigator, but he functions as a founding ancestor in living whakapapa and sits at the historical/legendary boundary — D-4 tension + taonga status; skipped, story censusable later with iwi consultation.
- **Taniwha** (census-flagged restricted): the generic class is extractable, but individual taniwha are iwi-specific kaitiaki (identity property); with only 2-3 beast slots I took Pouākai and Ponaturi, which carry no equivalent identity weight. Strong Wave-3 revisit WITH consultation.
- **Rongo, Kāne, Kū, Kanaloa, Taʻaroa, Tagaloa**: not dropped — folded into the syncretic groups of Lono, Tāne, Tūmatauenga, and Tangaroa as archipelago skins of one playable identity each (Tregear cognates); extracting them separately would double-count divinity wealth.
- **Ranginui, Papatūānuku**: cosmological scenery more than table players (their myth is fully carried by the separation record); natural vip_audience cameos, deferred to a later wave if wanted.
- **Tāwhirimātea, Rūaumoko, Hina, Laka, Haumea, ʻOro, Kamapuaʻa, Te Wheke-a-Muturangi, Moʻo, Patupaiarehe, Kurangaituku, ʻAumākua, Ngā Kete o te Wānanga**: real candidates lost to the 15-cap; Tāwhirimātea (the storm sibling who never surrendered) and Kamapuaʻa (Pele's rival, needed for her myth record) are my top two for any expansion slot. Ngā Kete overlaps the Tāne myth record and is census-restricted; ʻAumākua are living+restricted (family-private identities).
- **Io-matua-kore, Paikea, Hatupatu, Hinemoa, Nafanua, canoe captains, hālau-held chant repertoires, named iwi taniwha, tā moko**: closed/whakapapa/D-4 material per the census closed-material notes — reported, not collected, per the standing carve-out.
