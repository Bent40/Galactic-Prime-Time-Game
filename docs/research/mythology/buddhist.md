## 1. Tradition overview — the casino-frame angle

Buddhist cosmology is the mythic superstructure of a living major religion (>300M adherents). Its unique fit for the Cosmic Casino: **the tradition's own core teaching is that godhood is a bad long position.** Devas are rich, long-lived, and doomed — Itivuttaka 83 lists the five omens that appear when a god's merit runs out (garlands wither, clothes soil, sweat, dimming, restlessness), and this applies even to Shakra, king of the gods. Heaven is a high-roller suite you eventually get comped out of. The casino's thesis — divinity as a spendable, losable currency — is stated here as doctrine, 2,500 years early.

The extracted roster divides into natural casino departments:

- **Bodhisattvas** (Avalokiteshvara) — infinitely capitalized compassion investors playing *against* the house's cruelty from inside it.
- **Devas** (Shakra; the Four Heavenly Kings via Vaisravana) — incumbent management on expiring terms.
- **Dharmapalas** (Mahakala, Palden Lhamo) — oath-bound security staff; wrath as a service.
- **Wealth deities** (Vaisravana, Jambhala, Vasudhara, Benzaiten, White Mahakala) — an entire liquidity department; this tradition carries SIX of the census's luck_gambling flags, all extracted here.
- **The karmic bureaucracy** (Yama) — the audit office.
- **The fallen** — Mara, world religion's most famous all-in loser; pretas, beings who ARE bankruptcy, displayed at the buffet glass.

**Modern-life attribution (suggested, per canon §4):** the Buddhist pantheon plays as the casino's wellness-and-ethical-finance wing — mindfulness-app executives, microloan officers, an ESG compassion fund, one incorruptible audit office — all deadpan-corporate, and all quietly aware that the entire casino is the thing their own product teaches escape from.

**Founder handling (binding):** the historical Buddha is categorically excluded (census closed-notes + D-4). Several verified myths below include him as a participant (Mara's assault, Jambhala's shielding, Sucandra's inquiry). Wave 3 must stage him off-screen/unnamed — "the sage under the tree," a presence, never a character. Flagged per myth below.

Strong patron material deliberately left on the table to honor the task's luck/forsaken-flag priority within the 15-cap: **Tara** (rescuer from the eight fears — arguably the tradition's best patron deal-sheet), **Ksitigarbha/Jizo** (the vow to empty the hells; children/travelers), **Manjushri**, **Maitreya** (patron of every long game), **Amitabha** (the biggest open-door house in the cosmos), **Acala/Fudo**, **Hariti** (converted child-devourer — redemption-arc patron), **Brahma Sahampati**, **Marici**, **Skanda/Weituo**, **Vairocana**, **Bhaisajyaguru**. Beasts/spirits skipped: **Naga** and **Mucalinda** (rich; Garuda's counterpart — recommend pairing them in batch 2), **Yaksha**, **Gandharva**, **Kinnara**, **Asura**, **Rakshasa**, **Vajra** (artifact). Restricted-flagged census entries NOT extracted: **Yamantaka**, **Dakini** (public-iconography-only listing; owner review before any use). Excluded per census closed-notes: the historical Buddha and all deified-human figures (Padmasambhava, Milarepa, Bodhidharma, Budai, arhats), anuttarayoga tantra deities, Dorje Shugden.

## 5. Wave-4 cross-linking flags

Syncretic collisions to reconcile: **Benzaiten ↔ japanese_shinto** (Seven Lucky Gods census overlap; group `sarasvati_benzaiten` also touches hindu Sarasvati), **Mahakala/Daikokuten ↔ japanese_shinto**, **Yama ↔ hindu Yama / chinese Yanluo / japanese Enma** (group `yama_dharmaraja`), **Shakra ↔ hindu Indra** (group `indra_shakra`), **Vaisravana ↔ hindu Kubera** (group `kubera_vaisravana`; Jambhala deliberately kept OUT of that group — distinct deity in practice despite shared Kubera ancestry, relation `conflated_with` recorded instead), **Garuda ↔ hindu Garuda**, **Apsara ↔ hindu Apsara**, **Preta ↔ chinese egui / japanese gaki** (group `preta_hungry_ghost`), **Cintamani ↔ hindu/jain Chintamani**. Rubric outlier to review: Avalokiteshvara influence 5 (see §3.1).
