## The Demonic Brand — the contract (owner, 2026-07-15)

**The brand paradox is intentional: mercy earns the songs.** Branded parties skip the
Dissolution-songs encounter *by design* — complicity buys safety and costs you the route's
soul.

**What the brand actually is:** a **contract**. Demons don't touch you, you don't touch
them — and the reason it works is the point: *you're closer to them than you think.*

**How the immunity works (this is the key design):** the brand does NOT shield you from
demonic influence — it **dulls your emotions** so there's less to influence. A branded
character loses a massive amount of EQ. Everything — text, the way scenes are presented
from your point of view, the way you judge situations — becomes more pragmatic, less
emotional. You are harder to influence because there is less of you to reach.

### Digital implementation directions (sketch, not yet scoped)

1. **Presentation-layer effect (the flagship move):** branded characters literally *read a
   different game*. Same events, same facts — drier prose, emotional cues stripped from
   descriptions, NPC distress rendered clinically ("the vendor is upset" vs the unbranded
   player's full pleading dialogue). Dual-text content costs authoring effort but is
   mechanically cheap and thematically devastating. The player chose safety; the game
   quietly stops letting them *feel* the world.
2. **Mechanical hooks (numbers later):** slowed/immune Dissolution timers (existing rule);
   empathy-flavored tags (Fan Favorite, Mascot, Crowd's Baby…) drift faded / harder to
   earn; cold-read tags (Edgy, Menace, Corporate Asset) easier; audience reads the
   character as flat — spectacle scoring shifts from emotional beats to efficiency beats.
   Charm is NOT reduced (presentation still works) but its *register* changes.
3. **The contract cuts both ways:** attacking demons should breach it — define breach
   consequences when the Medium-route fork pass happens.

## Character IP split (owner, 2026-07-15)

- Most party characters (Filipe, XQUEZ/T, Mario, Sasha-as-played, Frod) are the **players'
  characters** — whether they appear in the video game or remain TTRPG-session-only is
  **UNDECIDED**. Do not build the game's premade contestants on them without an explicit
  owner decision (and, courteously, player buy-in).
- **The Nikita & Sasha story is KEEPER content** — the owner wants it in the game even if
  the characters must be rebranded/renamed. Treat the *story shape* (inherited fear, the
  two-state veteran, the reversion song, the scarf, the recognition asymmetry) as the
  protected asset; names/species/surface details are changeable.
