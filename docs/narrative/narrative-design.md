# Galactic Prime Time — Narrative Design Document

## Executive Summary

### Narrative Premise
Every quarter-millennium the bindings of the realms weaken and the gods claim divine
favor from a host realm's subjects — this cycle, humanity's. The world drops into the
**Cosmic Casino's** games at many tables at once; ours is a **VIP table** the gods built
from what fascinated them most about us: **reality television**. Contestants fight
through a broadcast dungeon while divinities wager on whether they break — and the
player, an **OC contestant**, is climbing a ~20-floor show where every floor-set stages
one moral question, every answer closes doors and opens others, and the finale is not an
escape but a **verdict**.

### Core Themes
- **The spine (canon):** *"How much can we break your essence down in the name of
  entertainment?"*
- **Essence vs. label:** what your choices reveal you to BE vs. what the audience calls
  you (tags) — kept deliberately separate and occasionally contradictory.
- **Complicity buys safety** (the brand contract): protection that costs you the
  capacity to feel what you're protected from.
- **Winning is joining:** divinity is a buy-in; the prize is becoming one of *them* —
  and the verdict decides what kind.
- Route-level sub-theme, F1–F3 (demoted from an earlier spine proposal): *"will you cure
  what's corrupted, and at what cost?"*

### Tone and Atmosphere
Showbiz over grief. The broadcast plane is comedy — announcer patter, absurd loot,
mocking achievements; the diegetic plane underneath carries the dread and the tenderness.
The tension between the two planes IS the tone: the funniest moments are someone's worst
day, on air. (Gods are **morally alien, never mustache-twirl evil** — hard rule.)

## Story Structure

### Story Type
Episodic-progressive with branching routes: floor-sets as "seasons within a season,"
each set = one staged moral question; **path-dependent unlocks** (routes open only to
those whose prior answers qualify); permadeath-adjacent stakes (recruited companions are
permanently losable). Structure: **question architecture** (canon), not acts.

### Act Breakdown (floor-sets)
- **Floors 1–6: designed** (content source: compendium campaign story + live campaign) —
  F1–F3 carry the corruption question; the Dissolution-songs encounter and the demonic
  brand contract sit in this arc (Medium-route forks + brand-breach rules: queued
  walkthrough, story-canon "open threads").
- **Floors 7–~20: paused by design** (canon) — each future set gets its question staged
  "in the most entertaining way possible." Question shapes ruled: *necessary vs right ·
  safety vs justice · power for yourself vs power for many.*
- **Finale: the verdict** — the show names what person you were and what **ruler**
  you'll be; canonically literal: the final winner shapes how history remembers the
  apocalypse for the next 250 years. Staging of the last episode: to design (rev-6 I-18,
  reduced scope).

### Story Beats (delivery unit)
The **episode beat**: every run segment is broadcast-shaped — cold open (Directive),
rising spectacle (hype), a Camera Call spotlight, a button (verdict-axis touchpoint or
recruitment). (Owner 2026-07-16: **adopted provisionally — try it, evaluate fit** during
floor authoring; drop if it fights the dungeon.)

### Pacing and Flow
Time skips between floors (the framework fast-forwards the host realm — gods skip the
boring parts). Combat fields carry the tense clock; the overworld and Lounge carry the
breath between. Noise/absorption keeps exploration narratively loud — sound draws story.

## Characters

### Protagonist
**The player's OC** (canon — RULED). Creation includes a background (origin, vice,
virtue, what you want back home ⟨PROPOSED picks⟩) that seeds starting **traits** and
draws **patron-god bids**. The OC's arc is emergent: the verdict axes are the character
sheet the player writes by playing. Player-OC death consequence: **OPEN** (Ascension-NG+
default until ruled).

### Antagonists
- **The framework itself** — the casino has no motive; it's a famine of attention with a
  house style. The true antagonist is the *question* each floor asks.
- **The fallen god running the table** (forgotten religion, "anything goes" license) —
  the house voice; host identity **OPEN** (Momus shared with the novel vs a sibling).
- **Patron gods as pressure** — a displeased patron's abandonment modes (extraction:
  "trials to max out on you even if you break" / total neglect) make your own sponsor a
  narrative threat without a contract exit. Buy-outs make success itself destabilizing.
- Floor-level bosses (Incinedile, demons, the Loong) are set-pieces, not the villain.

### Supporting Characters
- **Sasha** (RECRUITABLE, canon — name kept): cat; former stray who chose the safety of
  an old man's house over the streets and lives with the trade. The least threatening
  silhouette in the arena and the most dangerous thing in it. Crowd reads: mascot.
  Actually: constant exit-calculus, carrying someone else's war.
- **Nikita** (RECRUITABLE, canon — name kept): very old soldier; a war he never left, a
  scarf he can't return, a cat he won't be parted from. **Reversion** — the hummed song
  that brings War Nikita back (the priming showcase): 1945 peak, wartime logic, and no
  memory of the interval after. The show will learn the song works — and then the show
  will play the song.
- **Production cast: MISSING (rev-6 finding, OPEN)** — the announcer/host, the
  demographics department, recurring patron-god personalities. The patron roster (Ares/
  Enyo/Hestia stubs → real cast in KAN-7) is the cheapest place to grow it. ⟨PROPOSED⟩
- Other players' campaign characters: parked as concepts (canon) — usable later if wanted.

### Character Arcs
- **The keeper thread (canon, protected):** inherited fear (the maze truth — Sasha's
  fear isn't hers), the two-state veteran, the reversion song, the scarf, and the
  **recognition asymmetry** (War Nikita may not know his own cat; she alone can approach
  him) — its climax is the thread's crown jewel. Protected as *story shape*; surfaces
  small in the slice (she can approach War Nikita when no one else safely can).
- OC arc = verdict axes; companion arcs = authored, floor-gated scenes (Nikita's two key
  scenes: queued walkthrough).

## World and Lore

### World Overview
Full canon inventory: `../cosmic-casino-canon.md` (tables Normal/VIP/VVIP-Forsaken,
divinity economy, fallen-god runners, morally-alien gods). Game-first adoption (D3–D5):
world rules in, novel story out — Marcus/Viola/#3 are *examples*, never game content.

### History and Backstory
The quarter-millennial cycle is the engine of all human myth: **the winners of past
games decided how their apocalypses were remembered — every legend, idea, and
imagination stems from these games** (canon). This is load-bearing for gameplay: past
champions' myths are the **myth templates** players can recreate for epithets.

### Factions and Organizations
- **The house** — the fallen god's production apparatus; speaks only through Directives
  and System messages (the one legitimate in-fiction HUD channel).
- **God factions/pantheons** — relatedness graphs behind patron spill-over; affection
  ledgers make them long-term narrative relationships, not vending machines.
- **The audience tiers** — Viewers → Followers → Patrons (donator gods) → THE patron god.
- Dungeon factions (demons with the brand contract; town NPCs in the social layer) per
  floor content.

### Locations
Floors → districts/routes with exclusivity; the **Lounge** (comp suite — the house
always comps your room; surveillance = the house watching its assets); combat fields as
"on-air" zones. Arena canon: Incinedile P1 (41×60). Arena-as-psyche (the judge god's
mind shaping the space) is novel canon available to the game ⟨PROPOSED for later floors⟩.

### Cultural Elements
Epithets as the pantheon's comparisons ("Sasha the Nine-Lived" pattern); the crowd's tag
vocabulary (100 seeded) as pop-culture noise; casino diegesis for all economy language
(comps, markers, tips, the odds board).

## Dialogue Framework

### Dialogue Style
(RULED 2026-07-16) Broadcast lower-thirds + short trees + barks; no VN layer in v1. Two
registers per scene where planes diverge: what the audience hears (announcer) vs what
contestants experience. **Dual-prose for branded characters: post-MVP, set aside (owner
2026-07-16)** — the design remains canon (EQ loss = drier text) for later.

### Key Conversations
Recruitment encounters (offer/earn/join), patron deal sheets at creation (each suitor
god's voice), the notice-of-replacement beat (buy-out), Nikita's two key scenes (queued),
the Dissolution-songs encounter (mercy earns the songs — canon paradox).

### Branching Dialogue
Branches follow the question architecture, not dialogue trees for their own sake: choices
that classify the player feed verdict axes; social-director consequences arrive as world
manifestations and sudden quests (two-planes rule), not dialogue lectures.

### Voice and Characterization
Owner voices ruled so far: **Sasha cannot speak human — she talks to humans through the
CHAT FUNCTION** (the System's comms channel carries her meaning; audibly it's cat speak —
owner 2026-07-16); Old Nikita slow/precise/warm, apologizes to enemies; War Nikita clipped
imperatives, wrong decade's vocabulary, "comrade." Host: **Momus, shared with the novel
(RULED)** — never breathes between sentences.

## Environmental Storytelling

- **Visual:** the broadcast chrome IS diegesis (odds boards, hype bar as an on-air
  graphic); posture-swap silhouettes (Nikita's stoop/square); vents-and-exits geometry
  telling Sasha's story wordlessly.
- **Audio:** the hum — three notes and the whole arena goes still (canon signature
  beat); crowd as weather; tips arriving as stingers.
- **Found documents ⟨PROPOSED⟩:** past seasons' detritus — previous champions' merch,
  memorial plaques where contestants died, myth-template fragments doubling as epithet
  hints.
- **Environmental clues:** noise/absorption makes the soundscape tactical information;
  breach states visibly rewrite boss arenas (Incinedile canon).

## Narrative Delivery

- **No cutscene layer in v1 ⟨PROPOSED⟩** — the broadcast frame delivers: announcer VO
  (flagship want, host OPEN), System messages, world manifestations, sudden quests.
- **In-game storytelling:** the two information planes are the delivery architecture —
  spectators get dramatic irony, contestants get the world (canon; DIRECTION §director).
- **Optional content:** route-exclusive encounters, recruitment side-arcs, myth-template
  chases; Forsaken runs as a narrative *mode* (the gods' all-in — chosen, not refused).
- **Multiple endings:** one finale, many **verdicts** — the ending varies by essence
  axes + path history, not by a last-minute choice (canon: the instrument is the
  convergence matrix; content design queued as rev-6's top story item, W4/OPEN).

## Integration with Gameplay

- **Narrative-gameplay harmony:** every signature system is a story system — priming
  (Nikita's song IS a prime), hype (drama = economy), patron affection (relationships =
  buffs), permadeath of companions (loss = stakes), tags-vs-essence (theme = data).
- **Story gates:** floor-set questions gate routes; the brand contract gates the
  Dissolution-songs encounter (mercy earns the songs — skipping it is the *paid* path).
- **Player agency:** the verdict never overrides play — it measures it. The game must
  not moralize in text what it already scores in data ⟨PROPOSED principle⟩.
